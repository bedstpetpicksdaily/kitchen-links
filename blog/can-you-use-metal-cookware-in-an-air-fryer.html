Can You Use Metal Cookware in an Air Fryer?

Yes, you can use metal cookware in an air fryer, but with important caveats. Most stainless steel and aluminum cookware is safe, but certain metal types and configurations can interfere with air circulation or cause safety issues.

The Short Answer

Metal cookware is generally safe in air fryers as long as it's oven-safe and doesn't block airflow. Stainless steel pans, aluminum containers, and metal baskets designed for air fryers work well. However, you should avoid non-oven-safe metals, anything with non-stick coatings that aren't heat-rated above 400°F, and oversized pans that restrict the circulation of hot air around your food.

The Full Explanation

Air fryers work by circulating extremely hot air at high speeds around food to create a crispy exterior. This process requires unobstructed airflow, which is the primary concern when using metal cookware. Unlike traditional ovens, air fryers have limited space and rely on rapid air movement for proper cooking.

Safe Metal Cookware Options:

  • Stainless Steel Pans: These are excellent for air fryers. They withstand high heat, don't leach chemicals, and won't interfere with cooking performance.
  • Aluminum Containers: Both disposable and reusable aluminum pans are air fryer safe, provided they're rated for the temperatures your air fryer reaches (typically 350-400°F).
  • Air Fryer-Specific Metal Baskets: Many manufacturers sell replacement or additional metal baskets designed specifically for their air fryer models, ensuring proper fit and airflow.
  • Metal Skewers and Racks: These are perfectly safe and actually recommended for certain foods like kebabs or delicate items.

What to Avoid:

  • Cast Iron: While technically safe, cast iron heats unevenly in air fryers and can block airflow due to its weight and size.
  • Non-Oven-Safe Metals: If the cookware isn't marked as oven-safe, don't use it in an air fryer.
  • Damaged Non-Stick Coatings: Metal pans with peeling or degraded non-stick surfaces can release harmful particles at high temperatures.
  • Oversized Cookware: Pans that are too large for your basket will prevent proper air circulation and result in uneven cooking.

Key Considerations:

The size of your cookware matters significantly. Your pan should fit inside the basket with at least half an inch of space on all sides for air to circulate. If you're using a rectangular air fryer, try square or rectangular metal pans instead of round ones—they maximize space while maintaining airflow. Always check your air fryer's manual for specific recommendations, as different models have different specifications and safety guidelines.

Metal conducts heat efficiently, which is actually an advantage in air fryers. However, this also means metal pans will become extremely hot during cooking. Always use oven mitts when removing metal cookware from your air fryer, and be careful not to touch the pan's exterior.
