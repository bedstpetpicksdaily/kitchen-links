Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to pure carbon steel woks?

Pure carbon steel heats faster and develops superior non-stick seasoning over time, but it requires careful hand-washing and can rust if neglected. The Kuvera's stainless steel core trades ultimate performance for forgiveness—you can wash it normally without constant seasoning anxiety. It's the choice if you want wok cooking without obsessive maintenance.

Will this work on induction cooktops?

The stainless steel layer makes it technically induction-compatible, but the flat bottom can create hot spots and uneven heating on induction ranges. It's optimized for gas and electric coil stoves where the heating element contacts the pan more uniformly. Test it if induction is your only option, but it's not the primary use case.

How long does seasoning actually take?

Expect 5-8 cooking sessions (roughly 2-3 weeks of regular use) before the carbon layer develops meaningful non-stick properties. This is faster than pure carbon steel, but slower than pre-seasoned cast iron. After three months of regular use, most owners report that release matches mid-tier non-stick pans. Patience in those early weeks is essential.

Can you use metal utensils without damaging the seasoning?

Yes, carefully. The carbon infusion is durable enough for wooden spoons and metal wok spatulas, which is actually necessary for proper stir-frying technique. However, aggressive scrubbing with steel wool will strip the developing seasoning. Treat it like cast iron during the first few months, then it becomes more forgiving.
