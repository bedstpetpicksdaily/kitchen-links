Frequently Asked Questions

What nonstick coating lasts the longest?

Ceramic and PTFE (Teflon) coatings are the most durable options, with PTFE lasting 3-5 years with proper care and ceramic lasting 2-3 years. Ceramic is safer at high temperatures but scratches more easily, while PTFE is more scratch-resistant but requires lower heat to maintain longevity.

How can I make my nonstick pans last longer?

Hand wash your pans with soft sponges instead of dishwashers, use silicone or wood utensils instead of metal, cook at medium heat or lower, and store them with protective layers between stacking. Avoid preheating empty pans and never use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads.

Are expensive nonstick pans worth it?

Premium brands like All-Clad, Caraway, and Our Place typically last 5+ years compared to budget options that last 1-2 years, making them more cost-effective long-term. The better heat distribution and reinforced handles also provide better cooking performance, though mid-range options ($40-80) offer good durability without premium pricing.

Why does my nonstick pan stop working after a year?

Most nonstick pans fail prematurely due to high heat use, metal utensils scratching the coating, dishwasher damage, or stacking without protection. If your pan is new, the coating may be low-quality; if it's damaged, it's unsafe to use as the coating can flake into food.
