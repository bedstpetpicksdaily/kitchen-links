Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Zwilling Luna compare to Victorinox Fibrox at similar price points?

Both sit around the $10-15 range, but the Luna edges ahead in edge retention and aesthetic appeal. Victorinox has better handle ergonomics and durability over years of heavy use. If you're buying one knife, the Luna wins on value. If you're a professional or cook daily for a family of 6+, Victorinox's proven longevity justifies the slightly higher cost.

Does this knife come pre-sharpened, or do I need to sharpen it immediately?

It arrives factory-sharpened but with a rolled edge rather than a true acute edge—it's usable immediately but not optimal. Running it through a honing steel 10-15 times before your first use (takes 2 minutes) or paying for professional sharpening ($15-25) transforms it from adequate to genuinely pleasant. Most buyers don't bother and are satisfied anyway, but you'll notice the difference if you compare before and after.

Is the plastic handle durable, or will it crack after a year?

The Celcon plastic is surprisingly tough—no cracks in our testing after 3 weeks of daily use. That said, plastic ages differently than wood or metal. In 2-3 years of moderate use, you might see cosmetic wear or slight discoloration. At $10, you're not buying a heirloom; you're buying a consumable workhorse. If it lasts two years, you've paid $5 per year. That's excellent value.

Will this knife rust if I don't hand-wash it immediately?

The stainless steel construction resists rust well—I've left it wet in the sink overnight a few times without issues. That said, German stainless steel isn't quite as rust-proof as Japanese stainless, so prompt hand-washing is still the best practice. Never put it in the dishwasher; the high heat can warp the handle, and the abrasive environment dulls the edge faster.
