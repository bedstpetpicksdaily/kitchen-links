Frequently Asked Questions

Does the non-stick coating really hold up, or does it wear fast like cheaper brands?

The hybrid titanium-ceramic coating performs notably better than pure ceramic sets. After six months of regular use with hand washing, there's minimal visible degradation. The key difference is the titanium reinforcement—it resists the micro-scratching that kills pure ceramic within months. That said, this still isn't cast iron durability. One reviewer mentioned visible wear after two years of heavy daily use, so realistic lifespan is 2-4 years depending on care.

Is this set better than GreenPan's standard (non-Reserve) cookware?

The Reserve line uses a thicker aluminum base and improved hybrid coating compared to the basic GreenPan sets. Standard GreenPan costs less but shows faster wear on the non-stick surface. Reserve is the smarter investment if you use it regularly because the coating integrity extends another year or two. If you cook 2-3 times weekly, Reserve makes sense; occasional use means the basic line won't disappoint.

Can you really use metal utensils, or is that marketing language?

You *can* use metal utensils technically, but it accelerates coating damage. The hybrid reinforcement makes it more forgiving than pure ceramic, but regular metal utensil use will create visible scratches within months. Stick with wood, silicone, or nylon for longevity. If metal utensils are non-negotiable for your kitchen, cast iron or stainless steel is a better investment.
