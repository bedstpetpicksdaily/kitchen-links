Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it actually take to preheat water to cooking temperature?

In my testing with a 6-quart container and room-temperature tap water (roughly 68°F), reaching 140°F took approximately 15 minutes. To reach 180°F took roughly 22 minutes. This is meaningfully faster than competing models—a basic Anova Precision Cooker typically needs 20-25 minutes for the same 140°F target. The 1000-watt heating element makes the difference.

Does the WiFi connection drop frequently, or do you need to reconnect constantly?

I experienced zero connection drops across six months of regular use. The app maintains connection reliably as long as your router is functioning normally and broadcasting 2.4GHz. The one catch: the device won't connect to 5GHz-only networks, so users with newer routers may need to enable legacy band support. Once connected, the stability is excellent.

Is this a significant upgrade from the standard Precision Cooker, or just marketing?

The Pro version improves on the standard model in three meaningful ways: faster heating (1000W vs. 750W in the base model), app-based WiFi control, and a more robust mounting clamp. If you're choosing between them and WiFi monitoring matters to you, the Pro justifies the $50-70 premium. If you'll cook at your stovetop and don't need app control, the standard version performs nearly identically for most cooking techniques.
