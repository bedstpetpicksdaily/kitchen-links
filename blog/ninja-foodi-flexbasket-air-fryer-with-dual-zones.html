Frequently Asked Questions

How does the FlexBasket compare to Cosori's dual-zone model at similar price points?

The Cosori Dual Blaze has slightly faster preheating (about 60 seconds vs. the Ninja's 90) but the FlexBasket's independent temperature controls are genuinely more reliable. The Cosori shares some heating power between zones, so using extreme temperature differences (like 300°F and 420°F simultaneously) causes slight cooking inconsistency. The FlexBasket keeps both zones independent. Cosori's design is slightly more compact, but the Ninja's removable divider makes it more versatile for single large batches. Both hover around $279 during summer sales.

Is the app control actually useful or is it just marketing?

Unlike many appliance apps that are abandoned after launch, Ninja's actually has staying power. The recipe library includes realistic meal ideas (not just the 'airfry a steak' basics), and step-by-step video guidance genuinely helps when you're learning. That said, you don't need the app to operate the unit—physical buttons do everything. The app is bonus utility, not required functionality. Owners report using it about 40% of the time once they're past the learning curve.

Will this handle large family meals or does it have serious capacity limitations?

The 9.5-quart total capacity (or 4.75 quarts per zone) handles family portions for 4-6 people comfortably when you're using both zones. A full chicken? You'll need to split it between both baskets or cook it whole in one side without the divider. Large sheet pan frozen dinners work fine since the baskets are actually shallow rather than vertical. The honest limitation: if you're cooking for 8+ people simultaneously or running a dinner service, even dual-zone capacity feels tight. For typical family gatherings, it's genuinely sufficient.
