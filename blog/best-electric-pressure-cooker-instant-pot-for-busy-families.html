    Frequently Asked Questions

    What size Instant Pot should I buy for a family of 4?

    A 6-quart Instant Pot is ideal for most families of 4, allowing you to cook larger batches and meal prep efficiently. If you have a smaller kitchen or typically cook for fewer people, a 5.7-quart model works well and takes up less counter space. Anything smaller than 5.7 quarts may require cooking in multiple batches for family meals.

    How much time do Instant Pots actually save compared to regular cooking?

    Instant Pots typically cut cooking time by 30-70% depending on the recipe—dried beans cook in 30 minutes instead of 2 hours, and tough cuts of meat become tender in 30-45 minutes instead of hours. However, you should factor in 10-15 minutes for the pot to build pressure and natural release time, so total time savings are real but not instant. For busy families, the main advantage is that you can prepare a complete dinner in under an hour with minimal active cooking time.

    Is an Instant Pot worth it or just a kitchen gadget fad?

    For busy families, an Instant Pot is genuinely worth it if you meal prep, cook dried beans regularly, or want to make one-pot dinners on weeknights—it replaces multiple appliances (slow cooker, rice cooker, pressure cooker). However, if you rarely cook or prefer fresh ingredients and longer cooking methods, it may not be essential. The key is whether the time-saving features match your cooking style and schedule.

    What's the difference between Instant Pot models and which features actually matter?

    The main differences are size, wattage (higher wattage heats faster), and number of programs—basic models have 7 presets while premium models have 15+ including sous vide and sterilization. For busy families, you only really need pressure cook, sauté, and slow cook functions, so mid-range models like the Duo Plus offer the best value without unnecessary features. Avoid super budget models as they heat slower and have fewer safety features.
