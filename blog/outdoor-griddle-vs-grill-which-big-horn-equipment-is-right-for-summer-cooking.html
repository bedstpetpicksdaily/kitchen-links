Griddle vs Grill: Understanding the Key Differences

The primary difference between a griddle and a grill lies in their cooking surfaces. A griddle features a flat, solid cooking surface that distributes heat evenly across the entire area, making it ideal for foods that require a consistent temperature. A grill, on the other hand, has a grated surface with spaces between the bars that allow heat and smoke to rise directly onto your food, creating those signature grill marks and smoky flavor. Big Horn Outdoors designs their griddles and grills with precision engineering to ensure optimal heat distribution and durability, allowing you to achieve professional cooking results every time you fire up your equipment.

When to Choose a Griddle

Griddles are the MVP of breakfast and lunch cooking. From pancakes and eggs to grilled cheese sandwiches and sautéed vegetables, a griddle's flat surface is perfect for foods that might otherwise fall through grill grates. Big Horn Outdoors griddles are excellent for cooking smaller items, achieving a perfect sear on proteins, and preparing large quantities of food simultaneously. They're also ideal for stir-frying, making fajitas, or cooking delicate seafood that requires careful handling. The even heat distribution across the entire surface means no hot spots or cold zones—just consistent, reliable cooking performance.

When to Choose a Grill

Grills are the classic choice for summer barbecuing and are unmatched when it comes to imparting authentic grilled flavor and those coveted char marks. The open grates allow fat and juices to drip away, resulting in healthier cooking, while the direct and indirect heat zones give you more versatility for cooking different foods at different temperatures. Big Horn Outdoors grills are engineered for both high-heat searing and low-and-slow smoking, making them perfect for steaks, burgers, ribs, chicken, and vegetables. If you love that smoky, charred flavor that defines summer barbecue, a grill is definitely your best bet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use a griddle as a grill substitute?

While a griddle can cook many of the same foods as a grill, it cannot replicate the smoky flavor and char marks that make grilled food unique. However, Big Horn Outdoors griddles are excellent for achieving a perfect sear on meats and cooking foods that require precise temperature control.

Which option is easier to clean and maintain?

Griddles are generally easier to clean because their flat surface allows you to easily scrape off food residue. Grills require more detailed cleaning of the grates to remove grease buildup. Big Horn Outdoors designs their equipment with removable parts and easy-clean surfaces to minimize maintenance time.

Can I cook all summer foods on a griddle?

Griddles are versatile for many foods, but they excel at breakfast items, vegetables, and foods that require even heat. For traditional barbecue items like ribs and whole chickens, a grill provides superior results. Consider your typical menu when deciding which Big Horn Outdoors equipment fits your needs.

Is a combination griddle-grill a good option?

Yes! Many Big Horn Outdoors models offer combination griddle-grill surfaces, giving you the best of both worlds. This allows you to cook breakfast on the griddle side while grilling dinner on the other, making it a great investment for families who want maximum versatility.

What size griddle or grill should I choose?

Consider your household size and entertaining habits. Big Horn Outdoors offers various sizes to fit different needs. A general rule is to choose equipment larger than you think you need—outdoor cooking often involves cooking for more people than you initially anticipated.
