What to Look For

  • Adequate wattage and temperature range: Look for air fryers with at least 1400-1500 watts of power and the ability to reach 400°F or higher. Frozen foods require consistent, intense heat to cook through properly and achieve that crispy exterior without drying out the inside. Budget models sometimes skimp on wattage, which leads to uneven cooking and longer cook times.
  • Basket capacity between 3.5-5 quarts: For frozen meal cooking, you'll want enough space to cook a reasonable portion without overcrowding, which prevents proper air circulation. Too small and you're cooking in multiple batches; too large and you're paying for unnecessary size and energy consumption. This sweet spot gives you flexibility for family meals or meal prep without excess capacity you won't use.
  • Simple, reliable controls: Frozen foods benefit from straightforward preset options—you don't need WiFi connectivity or app control. Look for models with basic dial or button controls, clearly marked temperature and time settings, and ideally a shake-reminder feature for foods that benefit from mid-cooking agitation. These simpler controls are more durable and keep costs down.

What to Avoid

  • Ultra-compact models under 3 quarts: While tiny air fryers are inexpensive, they force you to cook frozen meals in multiple batches. For frozen food cooking where you're often prepping quick dinners, this defeats the purpose of convenience and wastes electricity running multiple cycles.   • Budget models without preset functions: The cheapest air fryers force you to manually adjust time and temperature for every single item.     Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you cook frozen meals directly in an air fryer without thawing?

    Yes, most frozen meals can be cooked directly in an air fryer without thawing, which is one of its biggest advantages. You'll typically need to add 5-10 minutes to the cooking time and may need to shake the basket halfway through for even cooking. Always check the package instructions as some items may require thawing first.

    What size air fryer do I need for frozen meals for a family of 4?

    A 5-6 quart air fryer is ideal for feeding a family of 4, allowing you to cook enough frozen fries, chicken nuggets, or other items in one or two batches. Smaller 3-4 quart models work for individuals or couples but require cooking in multiple batches for larger families. Consider your counter space and how often you'll use it before committing to a larger model.

    Do budget air fryers cook frozen meals as well as expensive brands?

    Budget air fryers cook frozen meals just as effectively as expensive models since they all use the same convection heating method. The main differences are typically build quality, durability, and extra features like preset buttons rather than cooking performance. For frozen meals specifically, a $50-80 air fryer will produce the same crispy results as a $200+ model.

    What's the best budget air fryer that won't break after a few months?

    Look for models in the $60-100 range from established brands like Cosori, Instant Pot, or COSORI that offer at least a 1-2 year warranty and have solid customer reviews for durability. Avoid the cheapest options under $40 as they often have heating element issues after light use. Read reviews specifically mentioning longevity and reliability rather than just cooking performance.
