Can You Use Regular Pots and Pans in an Air Fryer?

Yes, you can use regular pots and pans in an air fryer, but with important limitations. Most stainless steel and glass cookware works fine, but non-oven-safe materials and items with plastic handles should be avoided.

The Short Answer

Regular pots and pans can be used in an air fryer if they're made from oven-safe materials like stainless steel, glass, or ceramic. However, anything with silicone or plastic components, non-stick coatings rated below 400°F, or cookware not designed for high heat should stay out of your air fryer. The key is checking your cookware's maximum temperature rating and ensuring it fits your air fryer's basket without blocking airflow.

The Full Explanation

Air fryers work by circulating hot air at temperatures typically between 300-400°F (150-200°C), sometimes reaching higher. This means any cookware inside must withstand these temperatures without warping, melting, or releasing harmful chemicals.

What Works: Stainless steel cookware is generally safe since it handles high heat without issues. Oven-safe glass dishes and ceramic bakeware also work well, as long as they're rated for the temperatures your air fryer reaches. Cast iron is excellent for air frying—it distributes heat evenly and can handle any temperature your air fryer produces.

What Doesn't Work: Non-stick pans with plastic handles will melt. Traditional non-stick cookware often has temperature limits around 350-375°F, which may not be sufficient for all air fryer recipes. Cookware with silicone components, wooden handles, or decorative paint should be avoided. Additionally, oversized pans that block the air circulation basket defeat the purpose of air frying.

The Airflow Problem: Even if your regular cookware can handle the heat, it may be too large for your air fryer's basket. Air fryers need space around food to circulate hot air effectively. Cramming in a full-sized skillet can result in unevenly cooked food and wasted cooking potential.

What the Experts Say

Kitchen appliance experts recommend investing in cookware specifically designed for air fryers when possible. While regular oven-safe cookware works in a pinch, air fryer-specific cookware is sized appropriately to maximize the appliance's unique cooking method. Most manufacturers suggest keeping at least 1-2 inches of clearance between the pan and the air fryer's walls for optimal air circulation. Professional cooks note that the best results come from using cookware that balances heat conductivity with size—something air fryer-specific designs are engineered to do.
