What to Look For

  • Independent Temperature and Timer Controls: Each basket must have its own settings so you can cook frozen chicken at 400°F for 18 minutes while simultaneously air frying frozen broccoli at 380°F for 12 minutes without any compromise. This independence is absolutely essential—shared controls mean you're stuck cooking everything at the same temperature and time, defeating the entire purpose of owning a dual basket model.
  • Adequate Wattage and Heat Distribution: Look for models with 1700+ watts of power to ensure both baskets maintain consistent heat when running simultaneously. Underpowered units will struggle to reach proper temperatures when both baskets are loaded, resulting in unevenly cooked frozen foods that remain partially frozen in the center.
  • Basket Size and Spacing: Verify that each basket offers at least 4 quarts of capacity and that the baskets don't share heating elements or air flow paths. Poor spacing or shared ventilation means hot air circulation is compromised, leaving your frozen foods undercooked or with uneven browning, especially the items on the bottom layer.
