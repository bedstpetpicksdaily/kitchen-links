What to Look For

  • Independent Temperature Controls: The best dual basket air fryers let you set different temperatures for each basket. This is crucial because you might want chicken wings at 400°F while simultaneously cooking vegetables at 375°F. Models that force you to use the same temperature for both baskets severely limit your cooking flexibility.
  • Adequate Wattage and Even Heat Distribution: Look for units with at least 1700-1800 watts of power. Underpowered dual baskets struggle to maintain consistent heat between compartments, leaving you with unevenly cooked food. Check reviews specifically mentioning heat distribution—this is where many budget models fail.
  • Basket Size and Non-Stick Coating Quality: Each basket should hold at least 4-5 quarts to be genuinely practical for family cooking. Examine the non-stick coating reviews carefully; cheap coatings flake and degrade quickly with the high heat cycles that air fryers require, plus you'll want easy cleanup to actually use the appliance regularly.
