Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to Wüsthof or Victorinox?

Wüsthof runs slightly softer (around 56 HRC vs Zwilling's 56-58) and costs more—you're mostly paying for brand legacy. Victorinox Fibrox is the budget champion and holds an edge decently, but lacks the refinement in balance and steel quality. Zwilling sits in the middle: better steel than Victorinox, better value than Wüsthof. If you're debating between these three, ask yourself: am I cooking daily or occasionally? Daily cooks should stretch to Zwilling.

Is 8 inches actually better than 7 or 10?

Seven inches feels cramped for most adults unless your hands are small—you lose efficiency on larger cuts. Ten inches is overkill for home cooking and tires your hand unnecessarily. Eight inches is the Goldilocks length: enough blade to work through a whole onion in two cuts, maneuverable enough for precise brunoise. Japanese 210mm (8.2-inch) knives echo this preference for a reason.

Do I really need to hand wash it?

Yes. Dishwashers expose the blade to prolonged moisture, detergent residue, and rough contact with other items. Stainless steel resists rust better than carbon, but it still discolors and the edge can micro-chip. Hand washing takes 30 seconds. A 30-second habit preserves a multi-hundred dollar tool. Worth it.

How often should I sharpen this?

Depends on usage. Home cooks using it 4-5 times weekly can usually go 2-3 months before noticeable edge loss. Professional sharpening every 6-12 months keeps it pristine. At-home honing with a steel weekly maintains sharpness longer. Buy a decent honing steel first—that's half the maintenance battle.
