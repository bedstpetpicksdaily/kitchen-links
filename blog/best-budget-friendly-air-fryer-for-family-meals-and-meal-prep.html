What to Look For

  • Capacity of 6+ quarts: Family meals and meal prep require space. Look for air fryers with at least 6-quart capacity so you can cook chicken breasts, vegetable batches, or frozen fries for multiple people in a single run. Smaller models mean cooking in shifts, which wastes time and energy.
  • Even heating and reliable results: Budget models sometimes have hot spots or inconsistent cooking. Choose one with solid reviews specifically mentioning consistent browning and doneness across batches—this matters when you're prepping 20 chicken thighs for the week.
  • Easy cleanup and dishwasher-safe parts: When you're meal prepping for a family, the last thing you want is a complicated basket with crevices that trap food. Look for removable, dishwasher-safe baskets and trays that save you 10 minutes per cooking session.

What to Avoid

  • Avoid models under 4 quarts if you're feeding more than three people: Compact air fryers are tempting because of their low price tags ($40-60), but you'll spend your entire meal prep day cooking in batches. The time lost negates the money saved.
  • Steer clear of basket designs with fixed dividers or complicated removable parts: Some budget models try to add "features" like built-in racks or dividers that sound helpful but create more cleaning headaches. Stick with simple, open designs that maximize flexibility and cleaning speed.
