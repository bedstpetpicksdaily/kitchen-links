What Are Beeswax Wraps and How Do They Work?

Beeswax wraps are natural food storage solutions made from organic cotton fabric infused with beeswax, jojoba oil, and tree resin. When warmed by your hands, the wrap becomes moldable and conforms perfectly to bowls, produce, cheese, bread, and leftovers. The antimicrobial properties of beeswax naturally preserve food while allowing it to breathe, preventing moisture buildup that can lead to spoilage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do Bee's Wrap wraps actually last?

With proper care, Bee's Wrap wraps typically last for one year or longer. Proper care includes hand washing in cool water with mild soap and air drying flat. The lifespan depends on frequency of use and storage conditions.

Can I use beeswax wraps for hot foods?

Beeswax wraps are best used with cool to warm foods only. The wax can soften excessively with very hot foods. For hot foods, allow them to cool to room temperature before wrapping, or use alternative storage methods.

Are Bee's Wrap wraps safe for all food types?

Yes, Bee's Wrap wraps are safe for most foods including bread, cheese, vegetables, fruits, and leftovers. However, avoid using them with raw meat or extremely fatty foods, which can degrade the wax faster.

What's the cost difference between Bee's Wrap and plastic wrap over time?

While Bee's Wrap costs more upfront (typically $20-50 for a starter set), one set replaces approximately 300-400 rolls of plastic wrap annually. This means Bee's Wrap pays for itself within months while eliminating hundreds of plastic rolls.

Can beeswax wraps be composted?

Yes, at the end of their lifespan, Bee's Wrap wraps can be composted in home or commercial compost facilities. The cotton, beeswax, jojoba oil, and tree resin are all natural, biodegradable materials.
