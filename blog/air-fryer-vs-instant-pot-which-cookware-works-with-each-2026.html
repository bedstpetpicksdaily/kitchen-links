Air Fryer vs. Instant Pot: Which Cookware Works With Each (2026)

Air fryers and Instant Pots are both popular kitchen appliances, but they have fundamentally different cooking mechanisms—and that matters when it comes to cookware compatibility. An air fryer circulates extremely hot air at 350-400°F to create crispy results, while an Instant Pot uses sealed pressure cooking at up to 15 PSI. These different environments mean what works safely in one appliance may fail, damage, or pose a safety risk in the other.

If you already own cookware or are considering which appliance to buy based on your existing pans, understanding these differences is essential. This guide compares what cookware actually works with each appliance, the real limitations you'll face, and which setup gives you the most flexibility.

Stainless Steel Cookware (General Purpose)

Stainless steel pans and pots are the most versatile option across both appliances. In an air fryer, stainless steel handles high heat without degrading and won't release harmful fumes at 400°F. In an Instant Pot, stainless steel is the preferred material for the inner pot and can nest inside using a trivet if it has a flat bottom and fits within your model's capacity (typically 5.5 to 8 quarts).

The key limitation: regular stainless steel cookware cannot replace the Instant Pot's included inner pot during pressure cooking due to pressure and temperature engineering differences. However, you can use appropriately-sized stainless steel vessels as accessories for steaming or nested cooking with a rack.

Best for: Buyers seeking one cookware material that works reasonably well with both appliances, or who primarily use air fryers.

Non-Stick Coated Cookware

Non-stick pans are problematic for both appliances, though for different reasons. In air fryers, many non-stick coatings begin to degrade above 400°F and can release harmful chemicals. Additionally, metal utensils scratch non-stick surfaces, forcing you to use only silicone, wood, or plastic tools—limiting your cooking flexibility.

In Instant Pots, non-stick coatings degrade under the combination of high heat and pressure, and they can damage the appliance's sealing ring and heating element. Non-stick cookware is explicitly not recommended for pressure cooking use.

Best for: Not recommended for either appliance; better alternatives exist.

Glass and Ceramic Cookware

Glass and ceramic are oven-safe materials that can work in air fryers if rated for temperatures above 350°F. They don't react with acidic foods (unlike aluminum) and won't scratch. However, they take longer to heat and cool, and thermal shock (rapid temperature changes) can crack them.

Glass and ceramic are less ideal for Instant Pots because they're fragile under pressure and don't conduct heat as efficiently as stainless steel. They can be used as accessories with a trivet, but aren't a primary cooking solution.

Best for: Air fryer users who want an alternative to metal and don't mind slower heating; occasional Instant Pot accessory use only.

Aluminum Cookware

Aluminum conducts heat efficiently and works in air fryers at standard temperatures. However, aluminum reacts with acidic foods (tomato sauce, vinegar, citrus), potentially discoloring the pan and affecting food flavor. It's lighter and more affordable than stainless steel.

In Instant Pots, aluminum is technically heat-safe but not ideal for pressure cooking because it's softer and can warp under sustained pressure. It's rarely recommended for this use case.

Best for: Budget-conscious air fryer users cooking non-acidic foods; not a primary choice for Instant Pot cooking.

Instant Pot-Approved Inner Pot Alternatives

Some brands offer certified replacement inner pots designed specifically for pressure cooking that meet Instant Pot's engineering standards. These are the only 'regular' cookware option truly safe for pressure cooking inside an Instant Pot. They have the required thickness, pressure rating, and material composition to function safely.

These alternatives offer no advantage over the stock inner pot unless you need a backup or prefer a specific brand's warranty. They cannot be transferred to use in an air fryer or on stovetops without losing pressure-cooking capability.

Best for: Instant Pot owners who want a backup inner pot or prefer certified third-party replacements over stock equipment.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Stainless steel cookware works reasonably well in both air fryers and Instant Pots, reducing the need for duplicate sets.
  • ✓ Air fryers accept a wider variety of materials (metal, glass, ceramic, stainless steel) compared to Instant Pots, which require specialized pressure-rated cookware.
  • ✓ Metal utensils are safe in air fryers and won't damage the heating mechanism, unlike microwave ovens.
  • ✓ Air fryer cookware doesn't require the same engineering precision as Instant Pot cookware, making it more flexible and affordable.
Cons
  • ✗ No single cookware material is ideal for both appliances; Instant Pots have stricter requirements due to pressure cooking mechanics.
  • ✗ Non-stick coatings degrade in both appliances and restrict your utensil choices, making them a poor investment.
  • ✗ Regular cookware cannot replace an Instant Pot's inner pot during pressure cooking, regardless of material; you must use the stock pot or certified replacements.
  • ✗ Glass and ceramic are fragile under pressure and heat-intensive use, limiting their practicality for regular cooking.

Our Verdict

For air fryers alone: stainless steel and aluminum cookware offer the best balance of affordability, heat conductivity, and safety. You have flexibility to use glass, ceramic, and metal utensils—choose based on your cooking style and food types.

For Instant Pots alone: you must commit to the stock stainless steel inner pot or certified pressure-cooking alternatives. Regular cookware simply won't work safely, regardless of material quality. This is a non-negotiable design constraint, not a preference.

If you own both appliances: invest in a quality stainless steel set for your air fryer and accept that your Instant Pot will use only its designated inner pot. Trying to save money by reusing regular cookware in an Instant Pot creates safety hazards and appliance damage. The engineering constraints are real.

Bottom line: Air fryers are more forgiving about cookware variety; Instant Pots are highly specialized. If maximum cookware flexibility is a priority, an air fryer gives you more options. If you prioritize pressure cooking, accept that Instant Pot cookware is a dedicated ecosystem.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my regular kitchen pans inside an Instant Pot?

No. Regular cookware cannot be used as the main cooking vessel inside an Instant Pot during pressure cooking. The Instant Pot's inner pot is engineered specifically for pressurized environments (up to 15 PSI). Using incompatible cookware risks damage to the appliance, poor cooking results, and potential safety hazards. You can use certain oven-safe cookware as accessories with a trivet or steamer rack, but only for non-pressure cooking methods like steaming.

Can I use regular pans in an air fryer?

Yes, with important limitations. Regular cookware can work in an air fryer if it's oven-safe, rated for temperatures above 350°F, fits your basket without blocking air circulation, and won't release harmful chemicals at 400°F. Avoid non-stick pans with plastic handles that melt, and stainless steel or ceramic are generally safe choices. Always verify the cookware is labeled oven-safe at air fryer temperatures before using.

Are metal utensils safe in an air fryer?

Yes, metal utensils are safe in air fryers. Unlike microwaves, air fryers don't use electromagnetic fields that react dangerously with metal. Stainless steel tongs and spatulas work well. However, avoid sharp metal utensils if your air fryer has a non-stick coating, as they can scratch the interior. Always check your specific model's manual for manufacturer recommendations.

What's the main reason regular cookware doesn't work in Instant Pots?

Instant Pots operate under sealed pressure that creates an environment completely different from traditional cooking. Internal pressure rises to 15 PSI, and regular cookware isn't engineered to withstand this force without warping, leaking, or failing. The appliance's heating element, sealing ring, and pressure valve are calibrated specifically for the included pot. Using incompatible cookware compromises both safety and cooking performance.

Is aluminum cookware safe for air fryers?

Aluminum is safe for air fryer cooking at standard temperatures, but it reacts with acidic foods like tomatoes, vinegar, and citrus—potentially discoloring the pan and affecting food taste. It's lightweight and affordable, making it a budget option for non-acidic cooking. For regular use across different food types, stainless steel is a more reliable choice.

Can I use glass or ceramic cookware in an Instant Pot?

Glass and ceramic can be used as accessories in an Instant Pot with a trivet (for steaming), but they're not ideal for direct pressure cooking. They're fragile under sustained pressure, don't conduct heat as efficiently as stainless steel, and risk thermal shock from rapid temperature changes. Stick with stainless steel for primary cooking inside an Instant Pot.
