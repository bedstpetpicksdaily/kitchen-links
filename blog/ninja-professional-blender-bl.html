Pros
  • ✓ 1000-watt motor with genuine blending power—crushes frozen fruit, ice, and leafy greens into actual smooth consistency rather than chunky mush. The 72-ounce pitcher capacity means you're not refilling every 30 seconds for a family smoothie run.
  • ✓ Simple control panel with preset programs (smoothie, ice cream, frozen drinks) removes guesswork and actually works as advertised. No confusing 47-button interface to learn.

Cons
  • ✗ Motor noise level is legitimately loud (around 85 dB). If you're making smoothies before 8 AM with anyone else home, expect complaints. This isn't whisper-quiet operation.
  • ✗ Pitcher lids sometimes leak during blending if you overfill past the marked line. The 72-ounce capacity is real, but users report issues when treating it as a challenge.

Our Verdict

The Ninja Professional Blender BL earns its 4.3-star rating because it does one job exceptionally well: blending. It won't revolutionize your kitchen, but it will eliminate the frustration of weak blenders leaving you with chunky drinks. At under $150, it's priced fairly—not the cheapest option available, but meaningfully more capable than budget blenders. Buy it if you actually blend regularly (smoothies, soups, nut butters). Skip it if you're buying on impulse hoping it'll motivate you to eat healthier. Blenders don't change habits; they just execute on habits you already have.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Ninja Professional BL compare to Vitamix blenders?

The Vitamix costs 2-3x more and has slightly better build longevity (7-year vs. 1-year warranty typically). For everyday smoothies and basic blending, you won't notice a performance difference. Vitamix wins if you're blending hot soups regularly or need industrial durability. Ninja wins if you're budget-conscious and use it for standard home tasks.

Is the 1000-watt motor actually powerful enough for nut butters?

Yes, but with caveats. It'll make peanut butter or almond butter in 3-4 minutes with regular pausing. A 2000-watt Vitamix does it in 90 seconds. If nut butter is your primary use, you'd want something more powerful. For occasional batches, the Ninja handles it fine.

Will this handle hot soup blending?

It will, but don't expect it to heat the soup through friction like high-end models do. Blend cooled soup or let it heat separately. The pitcher is durable but not insulated, so blending boiling liquid risks burns. Many owners do this anyway—just be cautious about steam pressure buildup.
