What to Look For

  • Capacity of at least 8-10 quarts: Standard air fryers max out around 5-6 quarts, which means you're limited to cooking for 2-3 people comfortably. For families of four or more, you need a model that offers 8-10+ quarts of cooking space so you can prepare chicken breasts, vegetables, and sides simultaneously without crowding, which compromises crispiness and even cooking.
  • Multiple cooking zones or shelves: Look for air fryers with dual baskets or multiple levels that allow you to cook different foods at different temperatures at the same time. This is crucial when you're preparing a complete dinner—proteins, vegetables, and starches all have different temperature and timing requirements that work perfectly with multi-zone designs.
  • Reliable temperature consistency and powerful wattage (above 1500W): Larger capacity models sometimes struggle with uneven heat distribution. Choose models with higher wattage and proven temperature control to ensure that food in the back cooks as evenly as food in the front, preventing some family members from getting undercooked meals while others get overly browned portions.
