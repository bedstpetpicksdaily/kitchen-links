Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 10-quart compare to the 6-quart Duo Plus?

The 10-quart adds 4 extra quarts of usable cooking space and slightly more powerful heating elements to handle the volume. Both have identical cooking functions. The real difference: time-per-batch. A 6-quart might need two cycles to feed 8 people; the 10-quart does it once. Price difference is roughly $200-250, so the math only works if you'll actually use that extra capacity 2-3 times weekly.

Does it fit standard cabinet storage or does it need permanent counter space?

Most users report needing permanent placement. At over 15 inches in diameter and 12 inches tall with the lid, it doesn't fit standard cabinets. If you have pantry shelf space with 14+ inches of clearance, that works. Otherwise, plan for dedicated counter real estate or a kitchen cart.

Is this actually faster than a slow cooker for large meals?

Yes, measurably. A slow cooker's 8-hour braise takes 45 minutes in the Duo Plus at high pressure. You sacrifice the 'set and forget' convenience, but if you're cooking after work and need dinner in an hour, the pressure cooker wins. For all-day weekend cooking, the slow cooker function on this unit works fine and uses less electricity.
