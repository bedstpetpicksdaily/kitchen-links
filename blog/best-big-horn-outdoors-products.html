Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the seasoning step: Many new owners don't properly season their cooking surfaces before first use, which leads to rust and uneven heat distribution. Take 15 minutes to season your grates and surfaces with high-heat oil before cooking anything.
  • Overcrowding your cooking space: Just because you have the surface area doesn't mean you should use all of it at once. Leave space between items for proper heat circulation and to avoid temperature drops that extend cooking times.
  • Neglecting regular deep cleaning: Outdoor equipment collects debris and grease buildup quickly. Cleaning only between uses isn't enough—do a thorough deep clean monthly to maintain performance and prevent damage to moving parts.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Invest in a quality cover: Your Big Horn equipment will last significantly longer if protected from the elements. A fitted cover is one of the best investments you can make, preventing rust and UV damage that compromise both function and appearance.
  • Let it preheat properly: Unlike indoor cooking, outdoor equipment needs adequate preheat time to reach optimal temperature. Give your grill or griddle at least 10-15 minutes to stabilize before cooking—this ensures even cooking and better sear marks.
  • Keep a temperature log: Document how different heat settings perform in various weather conditions. You'll quickly learn that wind, ambient temperature, and humidity affect your cooking, and this knowledge makes you a more consistent outdoor chef.
