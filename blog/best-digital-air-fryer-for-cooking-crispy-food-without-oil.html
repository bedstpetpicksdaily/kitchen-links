    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do air fryers really cook food without oil?

    Air fryers use rapid air circulation technology to cook food with little to no oil, though many recipes call for a light oil spray for better browning and texture. You can achieve crispy results with just 1-2 teaspoons of oil compared to cups needed for deep frying, making it a genuinely healthier cooking method.

    What size air fryer do I need for a family of 4?

    A 5.8 to 6.5 quart capacity air fryer is ideal for families of 4, allowing you to cook enough food in one batch without overcrowding. Smaller 3-4 quart models require multiple batches, while larger 8+ quart sizes suit bigger families or meal prep.

    Can you really get crispy fries and chicken wings in an air fryer?

    Yes, air fryers excel at creating crispy exteriors on fries and wings because the circulating hot air removes moisture quickly. The key is patting food dry before cooking and using a light oil spray; results are often crispier than stovetop cooking and nearly match deep-fried texture.

    What should I look for in a digital air fryer?

    Look for adjustable temperature controls (ideally 170-400°F), a reliable timer, dishwasher-safe components, and a wattage of 1400-1700W for consistent cooking. A digital touchscreen or dial is easier to use than mechanical controls, and models with preset functions for common foods like chicken and fries save guesswork.
