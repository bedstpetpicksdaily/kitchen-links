    Frequently Asked Questions

    Should I use separate cutting boards for meat and vegetables?

    Yes, using separate cutting boards is the safest practice to prevent cross-contamination of raw meat bacteria onto vegetables that won't be cooked. If you only have one board, wash it thoroughly with hot soapy water between cutting raw meat and vegetables. Designate one board exclusively for raw meat if possible.

    What material is best for cutting meat and vegetables?

    Plastic and wood are the two best options, though they serve different purposes. Plastic is more hygienic for raw meat since it's non-porous and easier to sanitize in the dishwasher, while wood is gentler on knives and naturally antimicrobial for vegetables. Many home cooks prefer having a plastic board for meat and a wood board for vegetables.

    What size cutting board do I need?

    For a home kitchen, an 18x24 inch board is ideal as it provides enough space to safely cut larger pieces of meat and vegetables without crowding. If you have limited counter space, an 12x18 inch board works for most home cooking tasks, though you'll need to work in batches with larger cuts.

    Do I need a groove or juice groove on my cutting board?

    A juice groove is helpful but not essential if you're careful about meat placement. If you frequently cut raw meat and want to contain liquids, a groove prevents juices from dripping onto your counter and keeps your workspace cleaner, making cleanup easier.
