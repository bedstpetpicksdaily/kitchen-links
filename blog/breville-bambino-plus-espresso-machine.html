Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Bambino Plus actually compare to manual/lever machines for espresso quality?

The Bambino Plus uses pump-driven pressure (9 bars) versus lever machines that require manual pressure control. In practical terms: the Bambino delivers more consistent shots because temperature and pressure are electronically regulated. Lever machines offer more tactile control and can produce exceptional results in skilled hands, but require more technique. For most users, the Bambino's consistency wins. If you're into the ritual and precision tuning aspect of lever machines, you might miss that. For repeatable, cafe-standard shots without the learning curve, the Bambino outperforms.

Is 1.4 liters of water tank capacity actually a real limitation for daily use?

Depends on your routine. One double-shot espresso uses roughly 2 ounces of water; a cappuccino uses about 10 ounces total. If you're making 2-3 drinks daily, you're refilling maybe every other day or so. If you're making 5+ drinks or hosting regularly, yes, you'll notice it. The refill process takes 90 seconds max, but it's still an interruption. Solo users won't think twice. Households with multiple daily drinkers might consider this when evaluating whether 'compact' genuinely works for their situation.

What's the learning curve for dialing in grind and tamping pressure correctly?

The machine comes with a pressurized basket (single and double) which masks some dialing-in mistakes—water flows through even if your tamp isn't perfect. This is genuinely helpful for beginners; shots come out decent even during the learning phase. The non-pressurized baskets require more precision. Realistically: decent shots within a week, dialed-in shots after 2-3 weeks of daily use. The ThermoJet system actually helps here because consistent temperature means you're not chasing moving targets. Your grinder matters more than the Bambino's difficulty level—a decent burr grinder (even a $100-150 hand grinder) changes everything.
