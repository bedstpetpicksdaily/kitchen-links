What to Look For

When comparing microwave air fryer combos, focus on these four critical buying criteria:

1. Wattage and Dual-Function Power

Look for models with at least 1000W microwave power and 1300W+ air fryer capacity. This ensures both functions work efficiently without lag time. Lower wattage models compromise on performance and cooking speed, defeating the space-saving purpose.

2. Capacity and Interior Space

The best combos offer 25+ liters of usable space. Check the actual air fryer basket dimensions—some manufacturers inflate capacity claims. A model with at least 3.5 liters of dedicated air fryer space gives you realistic meal portions for 2-4 people.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use a microwave air fryer combo like a regular microwave?

Yes, most microwave air fryer combos function as standard microwaves for heating and cooking food normally. However, to use the air fryer function, you'll need to remove the turntable and insert the air fryer basket, so you can't switch between both functions simultaneously.

How much counter space does a microwave air fryer combo take up?

Microwave air fryer combos are typically 24-30 inches wide and 15-20 inches deep, similar in footprint to a standard over-the-range microwave. Some compact models are available for smaller kitchens, but they generally take up slightly more space than a traditional microwave due to the air fryer component.

Is a microwave air fryer combo worth it or should I buy them separately?

A combo unit saves counter or cabinet space and is generally more affordable than buying two separate appliances, making it worth it for small kitchens. However, if you have plenty of space and want maximum capacity for each function, separate units may perform better and offer more flexibility.

What's the best microwave air fryer combo for a family of 4?

Look for models with at least 1000+ watts of microwave power and a 3-4 quart air fryer basket capacity, such as the Instant Omni Plus or Ninja Foodi. These sizes handle family-sized portions for both microwave cooking and air frying without requiring multiple batches.
