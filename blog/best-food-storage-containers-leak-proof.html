Frequently Asked Questions

Are glass or plastic leak proof food storage containers better?

Glass containers are more durable and won't retain odors or stains, but plastic is lighter and more affordable for everyday use. For best results, choose glass for long-term storage and high-moisture foods like soups and sauces, and quality plastic for dry goods and lunch prep.

What makes a food storage container actually leak proof?

True leak-proof containers have airtight silicone or rubber seals on the lids that create a vacuum when closed, plus locking or snap-down mechanisms to keep the lid firmly attached. Avoid containers with simple clip lids, as they won't hold liquids reliably during transport.

Can I use leak proof containers in the microwave and freezer?

Most leak-proof containers are freezer-safe, but you need to check the label for microwave compatibility—many plastic lids or seals will warp at high temperatures. Glass containers with silicone seals are your safest bet for both microwave and freezer use.

How long do leak proof container seals last before they need replacing?

High-quality silicone seals typically last 1-2 years with regular use before becoming less effective at preventing leaks. Inspect seals regularly for cracks or hardening, and look for brands that sell replacement lids separately so you don't have to replace the entire container.
