What to Look For

  • Recipes with 15-minute or less active prep time: Look for dishes where you simply dump ingredients into the pot without complex chopping or browning steps.   • Meals that cook under 20 minutes on high pressure: When you're busy, every minute counts. Prioritize recipes that finish in 12-18 minutes of actual pressure cooking time, plus natural or quick release. Avoid recipes requiring 45+ minute cook times unless you're meal prepping on weekends.
  • Dishes that don't require a separate side dish: Choose all-in-one recipes where your protein, carbs, and vegetables cook together in the pot. This eliminates the need to manage multiple pans and means fewer dishes to wash when you're already tired.
