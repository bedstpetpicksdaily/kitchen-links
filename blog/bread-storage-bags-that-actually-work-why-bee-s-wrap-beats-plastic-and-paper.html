The Problem With Plastic and Paper Bread Storage

Plastic bags seem convenient, but they're problematic for bread storage. They trap condensation, which accelerates mold growth and makes crusts soggy. Meanwhile, paper bags work in the opposite direction—they allow too much air circulation, drying bread out within hours. Both options create waste, with the average household throwing away hundreds of plastic bags annually. For environmentally conscious cooks, neither is an acceptable long-term solution.

How Bee's Wrap Keeps Bread Fresh Naturally

Bee's Wrap works through a simple but brilliant mechanism. The beeswax coating creates a semi-permeable barrier that allows bread to breathe while retaining just enough moisture to maintain softness and texture. Unlike plastic, it doesn't trap excess condensation. Unlike paper, it doesn't allow moisture to escape too quickly. The result? Bread stays fresh 3-5 days longer than in plastic bags, and significantly longer than in paper. The organic cotton base provides structural support, while the plant-based oils add flexibility, making these wraps moldable around any loaf shape.

Can Bee's Wrap be used in the freezer?

Yes, Bee's Wrap works well for freezer storage. The beeswax coating remains flexible in cold temperatures, though you may need to slightly warm it with your hands to mold it around frozen items.
