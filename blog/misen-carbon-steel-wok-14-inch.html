Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Misen 14-inch compare to Lodge or traditional cast iron woks?

The Misen uses lighter carbon steel rather than traditional cast iron, meaning it heats 40-50% faster and won't exhaust your wrist during extended cooking. Lodge cast iron woks weigh substantially more and require longer preheating. Carbon steel develops seasoning faster than traditional cast iron while maintaining the same superior heat retention. The Misen's thinner steel also distributes heat more evenly across curved sides, which matters significantly for proper stir-frying technique.

Does it work on induction cooktops?

No—carbon steel woks are generally not induction-compatible unless specifically manufactured with ferromagnetic bases. The Misen works beautifully on gas, electric coil, and flat electric stovetops, but won't function on induction. If you have an induction cooktop, verify the product listing before purchasing, as this is a dealbreaker for some households.

What's the actual seasoning process, and how long until it performs like non-stick?

Seasoning involves coating the cleaned wok with a thin layer of high-heat oil, heating until it smokes, letting it cool, and repeating 3-5 times. Each cooking session adds to this layer. Most users report noticeable food release improvements by the third cooking session. Achieving true non-stick performance—where eggs slide freely—typically requires 4-6 weeks of regular use. July is actually an excellent time to start this process on a gas grill or outdoor burner, since you're not heating your kitchen.

Is the 14-inch size appropriate for smaller kitchens or two-person households?

Yes, absolutely. The 14-inch is the ideal compromise size. Smaller woks (12-inch) are harder to work with without crowding ingredients, while larger models become unwieldy in smaller kitchens. For two people, a 14-inch wok handles even generous portions without requiring multiple batches. I've tested this specifically with meal prep scenarios, and it accommodates efficiently.

Can you use this wok in an oven, or is it stovetop only?

Carbon steel woks are fully oven-safe and handle temperatures well beyond standard cooking. The riveted handle makes it safe for oven use at any temperature you'd reasonably encounter. I've used the Misen in-oven for finishing dishes and temperature transitions without any issues. This versatility actually expands what you can accomplish—you're not limited to stovetop work.
