Frequently Asked Questions

Does the WiFi connection drop often?

Based on user feedback, it's stable on modern dual-band routers but can hiccup on older 2.4GHz-only networks. Update your router firmware before blaming the cooker. The Anova still works perfectly without WiFi—you just lose the remote convenience.

What pot size should I use with this?

Works best in 3-5 gallon containers (11-19 liters). Smaller pots lose heat too quickly; larger ones take forever to come to temp. A basic stainless steel stockpot from any kitchen supplier is perfect and costs $20-30. Anova sells branded containers too, but you don't need them.

Is this better than other WiFi immersion circulators?

The Anova Pro holds its own against Joule and SANSAIRE at this price tier. Joule heats slightly faster but costs more and has a smaller water capacity. SANSAIRE is cheaper but feels less refined. For actual cooking results, the differences are marginal—this is about interface and reliability, where Anova delivers solidly.

How long before vacuum-sealed food starts degrading in the water bath?

Most sous vide cooks finish within 2-4 hours. Beyond that, you can hold food safely for another 30-45 minutes with temperature maintained. If you're doing longer 24+ hour cooks (like tough cuts or eggs), circulation quality matters more—this unit handles that without issue.
