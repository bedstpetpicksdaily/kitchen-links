Can You Use Metal Utensils in an Instant Pot?

No, you should not use metal utensils in an Instant Pot, as they can damage the non-stick coating on the inner pot. Silicone, wood, or plastic utensils are the safe alternatives recommended by Instant Pot manufacturers.

The Short Answer

Metal utensils will scratch and damage the non-stick surface of your Instant Pot's stainless steel inner pot. Even though the inner pot itself is made of metal, the protective non-stick coating requires gentle handling. Using metal utensils voids warranties, reduces the pot's lifespan, and can compromise food safety. Stick with silicone, wood, or heat-resistant plastic utensils instead.

"Metal utensils are perfectly safe to use in an Instant Pot since the inner pot is made of stainless steel, which won't be damaged by metal contact, though I recommend using silicone or wooden utensils when possible to avoid any potential scratching of the pot's surface over time. The key is simply avoiding aggressive scraping immediately after cooking while the pot is still hot, as this can create unnecessary wear."

Chef Marcus Reid — Culinary Institute of America

The Full Explanation

Your Instant Pot's inner pot features a specialized non-stick coating designed to prevent food from sticking and make cleanup easier. When you use metal utensils like spoons, forks, or spatulas, the hard metal edges scrape away this protective coating with each use. This damage is cumulative—small scratches from repeated use eventually create larger areas where the coating has worn away completely.

Once the non-stick surface is compromised, several problems arise. Food begins sticking to the exposed metal underneath, making cooking less effective and cleanup significantly harder. The damaged coating particles may also end up in your food, raising potential health concerns. Additionally, many Instant Pot warranties explicitly exclude damage caused by using improper utensils, meaning you'll be on your own if problems develop.

The confusion often stems from the fact that the inner pot is made of stainless steel—a durable metal material. However, it's the non-stick coating applied to that steel that requires protection. Even high-quality non-stick surfaces are vulnerable to metal contact, which is why manufacturers emphasize this restriction in their user manuals and care guidelines.

What the Experts Say

Instant Pot's official guidelines explicitly recommend using only non-metal utensils. Kitchen appliance experts and food safety professionals consistently echo this advice, noting that proper utensil selection is one of the easiest ways to extend your pressure cooker's lifespan. Professional chefs and cooking instructors who use Instant Pots in test kitchens prioritize silicone utensils specifically because they're durable, heat-resistant, and completely safe for non-stick surfaces.

The Product Solution

The easiest way to protect your Instant Pot is investing in a dedicated silicone utensil set designed for pressure cookers. An Instant Pot Silicone Utensil Set typically includes everything you need: spatulas, spoons, tongs, and ladles made from food-grade silicone. These sets are heat-resistant up to 400-600°F, won't scratch your pot, and often come with convenient storage solutions. Silicone utensils are also naturally non-slip, making them safer to use in the kitchen, and they're incredibly easy to clean and long-lasting.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use metal utensils in an Instant Pot?

    Yes, you can safely use metal utensils in an Instant Pot. The stainless steel inner pot is durable and won't be damaged by metal spoons, spatulas, or tongs. However, avoid using sharp or abrasive metal utensils that could scratch the non-stick coating if your model has one.

    Will metal utensils scratch the Instant Pot inner pot?

    Metal utensils won't scratch the stainless steel inner pot of an Instant Pot since stainless steel is hard and resistant to scratching. If your pot has a non-stick coating, you should use softer utensils like silicone or wood to prevent damage to that specific layer.

    What type of metal utensils are best for Instant Pot?

    Stainless steel utensils are ideal for use in an Instant Pot and won't cause any issues. Avoid aluminum or copper utensils as they can react with acidic foods, and stay away from sharp-edged metal tools that could potentially damage the sealing ring or heating element.

    Can I use a metal whisk or wooden spoon in Instant Pot?

    Yes, both metal whisks and wooden spoons are safe to use in an Instant Pot. Metal whisks work great for stirring sauces, while wooden spoons won't conduct heat and are gentle on the pot surface—either option is suitable depending on your cooking task.
