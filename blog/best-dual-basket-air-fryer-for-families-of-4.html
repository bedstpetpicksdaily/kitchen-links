What to Look For

  • Combined capacity of 8-10 quarts: This gives you enough room to prepare proteins and vegetables for four people in one cooking cycle, or split tasks between baskets—say, chicken wings in one basket and roasted broccoli in the other. Anything less feels cramped for family portions; anything more wastes energy and takes up too much counter real estate.
  • Independent temperature and timer controls: Since family meals rarely feature ingredients that cook identically, you need separate controls for each basket. This lets you cook salmon at 380°F for 12 minutes while simultaneously air frying french fries at 400°F for 15 minutes without compromise.
