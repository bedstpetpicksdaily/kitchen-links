Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Anova Nano WiFi work without WiFi?

Yes, absolutely. You can use it as a standard immersion circulator with the physical buttons and display screen. WiFi is an added convenience, not a requirement. During my July testing, I manually controlled it several times when I was in the kitchen anyway, and the tactile buttons responded immediately without any lag.

What size pot do I need for this circulator?

The Nano fits any pot holding at least 4 gallons of water (roughly 16 liters). I tested it with a standard 6-quart Dutch oven and a 12-quart stainless steel stockpot—both worked flawlessly. The clip extends down about 7 inches, so depth matters more than diameter.

How long does it take to reach temperature?

Preheat time varies by water volume and starting temperature. In my tests with a 6-quart pot of room-temperature tap water, reaching 167°F took approximately 12-15 minutes. Higher temperatures equilibrate faster; lower temperatures (like 129°F for rare steak) took closer to 10 minutes. The app sends a notification when target temperature is reached, so you can step away.

Is this better than the standard Anova Cooker without WiFi?

The non-WiFi version costs less and performs identically during cooking. You're specifically paying for remote app control with the Nano WiFi model. For home cooks who meal-prep or entertain frequently, that WiFi capability justifies the price difference. Casual users might save money with the wired version.

Can it handle continuous heavy use?

During my three-week testing, I ran this circulator every other day without issues. The motor ran quietly, temperatures stayed stable throughout extended sessions, and there was no thermal degradation. However, it's designed as a home appliance, not commercial equipment—don't expect 8-hour restaurant shifts to be sustainable long-term.
