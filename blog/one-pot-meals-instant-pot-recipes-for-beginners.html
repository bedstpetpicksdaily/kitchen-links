What to Avoid

  • Skip the Ultra Model (for now): The Instant Pot Ultra has more features and customization than beginners need, adding complexity without improving results on simple recipes. Save the learning investment for when you're comfortable with pressure cooking fundamentals.
  • Don't Ignore the Liquid-to-Food Ratio: A common beginner mistake is adding too much liquid "to be safe," which waters down flavors and creates mushy textures. Follow tested recipes exactly the first few times to understand proper ratios before experimenting.
  • Avoid Thickening During Pressure Cooking: Using cornstarch, flour, or cream during the pressure phase creates lumps and clogs the vent system. Wait until after pressure cooking to thicken sauces—this single lesson prevents most beginner frustrations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need an Instant Pot or can I use a regular pressure cooker?

While a regular stovetop pressure cooker works, an Instant Pot is much safer and easier for beginners since it has automatic pressure release, preset cooking programs, and built-in safety features. The Instant Pot also doubles as a slow cooker and rice cooker, making it more versatile for one-pot meals.

How much liquid do I need for Instant Pot recipes?

Most Instant Pot recipes require a minimum of 1 cup of liquid to create steam and build pressure, though some recipes need up to 2 cups depending on cooking time. You'll need less liquid than stovetop cooking since the sealed environment prevents evaporation.

Can I convert my regular one-pot recipes to Instant Pot recipes?

Yes, but you'll need to reduce the cooking time by about 30% and use slightly less liquid than stovetop recipes. Keep in mind that foods like pasta and delicate vegetables may need special handling, so checking beginner-friendly conversion guides is helpful when adapting recipes.

What size Instant Pot should I buy for a family of 4?

A 6-quart Instant Pot is ideal for a family of 4 as it gives you flexibility to cook larger portions and batch meals for leftovers. The 3 or 5-quart sizes work too but may feel limiting when cooking for multiple people or meal prepping.
