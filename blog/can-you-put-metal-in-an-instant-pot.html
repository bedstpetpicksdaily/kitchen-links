Can You Put Metal in an Instant Pot? The Direct Answer

Yes, you can put certain types of metal in an Instant Pot, but not all metal is safe. Stainless steel cookware and utensils are generally safe, while aluminum and non-stick coated metals should be avoided as they can damage the pot's interior or leach chemicals into your food.

The Short Answer

The Instant Pot's inner pot is made of stainless steel, which is designed to withstand contact with other metal objects. However, you need to be selective about which metals you use. Stainless steel utensils, metal steamers, and trivet accessories are all safe choices. Avoid reactive metals like aluminum, copper, and anything with non-stick coatings, as these can scratch the pot's surface or contaminate your food with harmful substances.

Always verify that any metal cookware or accessories are specifically labeled as Instant Pot-compatible before purchasing, since not all metals can withstand the high pressure and heat without damaging the pot's interior or affecting cooking performance. Check the manufacturer's guidelines to confirm whether stainless steel inserts, metal trivets, or other accessories are safe for your specific Instant Pot model.

The Full Explanation

Understanding what metal is safe in your Instant Pot requires knowing both the pot's construction and how different metals interact with it. The Instant Pot's inner cooking vessel is made of food-grade stainless steel, which is durable and resistant to corrosion. This means the pot itself won't degrade from contact with other stainless steel items.

Safe Metal Items for Your Instant Pot:

  • Stainless steel mixing bowls and measuring cups
  • Metal trivets and steamer baskets (usually included with Instant Pot models)
  • Stainless steel utensils like spoons and tongs
  • Metal insert pans designed for pressure cooking
  • Stainless steel springform pans for cheesecake and baking

Metal Items to Avoid:

  • Aluminum cookware or foil (reacts with acidic foods)
  • Copper or copper-bottomed pans
  • Non-stick coated metal items (coating can scratch and flake)
  • Cast iron (can rust and discolor food in a humid pressure environment)
  • Painted or decorative metal items (paint can chip or leach toxins)

The key principle is that stainless steel is inert and food-safe, while other metals can either react with your food—especially acidic ingredients like tomato sauce or citrus—or damage the pot's protective surface. When the protective stainless steel layer is compromised, the underlying material may leach into your food or the pot itself may corrode over time.

Metal utensils are perfectly fine to use when stirring or removing food from your Instant Pot. Many home cooks worry about scratching the interior, but the stainless steel is thick enough to handle normal utensil contact without problems. However, avoid aggressive scraping or dragging sharp objects across the bottom of the pot when it's empty, as this can create unnecessary wear.

What the Experts Say

According to the official Instant Pot documentation and culinary experts, stainless steel is the gold standard for pressure cooking because it doesn't react with food and distributes heat evenly. Pressure cooking specialists recommend avoiding any metal that isn't food-grade stainless steel, as the high temperature and pressure environment can accelerate chemical leaching from reactive metals. Professional chefs and kitchen equipment reviewers consistently emphasize that investing in proper stainless steel accessories is worth the cost for both safety and longevity of your Instant Pot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you put metal utensils in an Instant Pot?

Yes, you can use metal utensils like spoons, tongs, and spatulas in your Instant Pot without damaging it. The stainless steel inner pot is designed to withstand metal contact, so there's no risk of scratching or ruining your cooker.

Is it safe to use metal steamer racks in an Instant Pot?

Absolutely, metal steamer racks and trivets are safe and commonly used in Instant Pots for cooking. In fact, most Instant Pot models come with a metal trivet included, making them perfect for steaming and elevating food above liquid.

Can you use metal pots or pans inside an Instant Pot?

Yes, you can use stainless steel pots, pans, and containers that fit inside your Instant Pot for cooking or steaming. Just make sure they're oven-safe and fit comfortably without touching the heating element on the sides.

Will metal cookware scratch the Instant Pot inner pot?

Metal utensils and cookware won't scratch the Instant Pot's stainless steel inner pot under normal cooking conditions. The inner pot is specifically designed with a durable finish that resists scratching from metal contact.
