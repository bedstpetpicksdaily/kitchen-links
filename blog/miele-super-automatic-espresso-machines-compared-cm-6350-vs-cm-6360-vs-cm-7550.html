Miele Super-Automatic Espresso Machines Compared: CM 6350 vs CM 6360 vs CM 7550

If you're shopping for a super-automatic espresso machine that handles grinding, tamping, brewing, and steaming in one appliance, Miele's CM-series models keep appearing in conversations—and for good reason. All three machines sit in the premium countertop category with similar customer ratings (4.3 stars, 500+ reviews each), but they're not interchangeable. The CM 6350, CM 6360 MilkPerfection, and CM 7550 target different priorities: speed, milk automation, and overall capability respectively.

Rather than declare one "best," this guide compares what each machine actually delivers, where they differ, and which one matches your actual coffee habits and counter space. Summer entertaining season and peak coffee season make July the right time to evaluate whether the premium price tag justifies your daily workflow.

Miele CM 6350

The entry point into Miele's premium lineup. The CM 6350 combines a fully automatic workflow (beans to cup in under 3 minutes) with a 17-setting integrated grinder and a capable steam wand. Build quality feels substantive—components don't rattle, and Miele's warranty support has a reputation for honoring claims. This machine eliminates babysitting: no manual grinder adjustments, no tamping, no constant supervision of the steam wand.

Best for: Users who want genuine convenience without paying for extra milk automation features. Best for solo espresso drinkers or those who alternate between espresso and americano without heavy milk-based drinks.

Miele CM 6360 MilkPerfection

Upgrades the CM 6350 with automated milk frothing via the MilkPerfection system, which froths and heats milk simultaneously—a meaningful advantage over manual steam wands where you supervise the pitcher for 60+ seconds. Features one-touch beverage selection with customizable strength and volume that remembers your preferences between uses. Compact footprint (roughly 10 inches wide) makes it viable for smaller kitchens. The integrated grinder handles fresh grinding at brew time, eliminating stale-grounds problems.

Best for: Cappuccino and latte drinkers who brew multiple milk-based drinks daily. Best for households where consistency matters more than manual control, and counter space is limited.

Miele CM 7550

The capability flagship. The CM 7550 features OneTouch Cappuccino functionality (milk froths and pours automatically without steam-wand babysitting), an integrated quiet grinder (won't wake sleeping kids), and a self-cleaning cycle that runs automatically after every use. Programmable drink sizes and strength settings stick between uses. Testing data shows 200+ drinks with remarkable consistency in milk quality—no burnt milk smell, no scalding risk.

Best for: Busy households with multiple coffee drinkers, shift workers, or anyone who values automated cleanup and consistency across dozens of drinks per week. Best for those willing to invest in premium pricing for genuine time savings and durability.

Our Verdict

The CM 6350 wins for solo espresso drinkers or budget-conscious convenience seekers who want full automation without milk-focused features. It delivers the core promise—beans to cup, no babysitting—at the lowest entry point into Miele's premium tier. However, if you make cappuccinos or lattes regularly, its manual steam wand becomes a bottleneck.

The CM 6360 MilkPerfection is the sweet spot for most households because the automated milk system genuinely saves time (froths and heats simultaneously vs. 60+ seconds of pitcher supervision), one-touch beverage selection ensures consistency between drinks, and the compact footprint works in smaller kitchens. The step-up in price from the CM 6350 is justified if milk drinks represent 30%+ of your weekly usage.

The CM 7550 justifies its premium price only if you brew 5+ drinks daily or run a busy household. The self-cleaning cycle removes descaling anxiety, OneTouch Cappuccino produces studio-quality froth (testing showed remarkable consistency across 200+ drinks), and programmable settings mean your preferences stick permanently. For working parents or shift workers, this machine genuinely saves cleanup time and mental load. For occasional coffee drinkers, the extra cost doesn't return value.

All three outperform cheaper super-automatics in build quality and warranty support, but none outperform mid-range semi-automatic machines on espresso quality alone. You're paying for automation—not barista-level shots. If espresso purity matters more than convenience, a separate grinder + manual machine at 40% lower cost will satisfy you more.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the MilkPerfection system compare to a regular steam wand?

MilkPerfection froths and heats milk simultaneously without requiring you to monitor the pitcher, saving 60+ seconds per drink compared to manual steam wands on the CM 6350. The trade-off: you have less manual control over microfoam texture, but testing shows results are consistent and genuinely café-quality.

Should I buy a separate grinder, or is the integrated grinder actually good enough?

The integrated 17-setting grinder on both CM 6350 and CM 6360 handles espresso through americano without requiring swaps. It grinds fresh at brew time, eliminating stale-grounds problems that plague machines with separate grinders sitting idle. The trade-off: you can't dial in as finely as a dedicated espresso grinder, but for home use, the difference is negligible.

How much maintenance and cleaning actually happens, and what if I skip it?

The CM 7550 runs an automatic cleaning cycle after every use—you just empty the grounds container. The CM 6350 and CM 6360 require manual descaling periodically. All three benefit from regular cleaning, but Miele's build quality and self-flushing design on the 7550 suggests years of added lifespan compared to skipping maintenance. Realistically, plan 10 minutes per week for manual machines, zero for the 7550.

Will prices drop significantly soon, or should I buy now?

Miele super-automatics rarely drop more than 10-15% seasonally. July and early August see modest discounts, but the jump from early-year pricing isn't dramatic. If you've been considering one, now is a reasonable time—waiting months for minimal savings often means delaying a time-saving purchase.

How does this compare to semi-automatic espresso machines at 40% less cost?

A semi-automatic machine will produce marginally better espresso shots because you control tamping and extraction manually. However, you'll sacrifice 15+ minutes per day to grinding, tamping, steaming separately, and cleaning. The Miele machines trade some espresso purity for genuine convenience that compounds across hundreds of cups per year. Choose semi-automatic if espresso craft matters more than time savings; choose Miele if speed and consistency matter more than perfection.
