Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 6-quart size actually make a practical difference versus the 5-quart models?

Yes, measurably so. The extra quart allows you to fill past the halfway mark for certain dishes like soups and stews where the 5-quart gets cramped. I found myself able to cook a full pound of dried beans with room for ingredients, whereas the 5-quart forces you into two batches. For families or meal preppers, this single-liter difference compounds across an entire cooking season.

How loud is the steam release, and will it disturb people sleeping nearby?

The quick release produces a sustained hiss that reaches about 85 decibels—comparable to a blender. Natural release is silent. If you're cooking late at night in a small apartment, quick release will announce itself. I'd recommend timing large meals for when noise is less of a concern, or embrace natural release and plan accordingly into your cooking schedule.

Can you really replace multiple kitchen appliances with this single unit, or is that marketing hype?

Partially true, honestly. The Duo EVO genuinely replaces a dedicated pressure cooker, slow cooker, and rice cooker. I stopped using my crockpot entirely. However, it won't replace your instant-read thermometer, cutting board, or stovetop completely—some tasks still benefit from traditional methods. Think of it as consolidating three appliances into one, not eight into one. That's still genuinely useful, especially for July kitchens where counter space is premium during summer cooking season.
