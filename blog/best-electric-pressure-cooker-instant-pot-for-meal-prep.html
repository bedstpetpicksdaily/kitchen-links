What to Look For

  • Capacity between 6-8 quarts: Meal prep demands volume. A smaller 3-quart model forces you into multiple batches, defeating the purpose of efficient cooking. The 6-8 quart range lets you cook for 4-6 people or multiple meal prep containers in a single cycle, saving you hours throughout the week.
  • Stainless steel inner pot with clear markings: You'll be filling this pot frequently and need to see exactly how full it is to prevent overflow or insufficient liquid. Stainless steel resists staining and odors better than non-stick options, crucial when meal prepping aromatic ingredients like curry or chili repeatedly.
  • Multiple cooking programs plus manual pressure control: Meal prep variety demands flexibility. Look for preset programs for beans, grains, meat, and vegetables, but also manual pressure settings so you can customize cooking times for your specific recipes without guesswork.
