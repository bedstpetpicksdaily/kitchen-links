Pros
  • ✓ 5.8L capacity gives you meaningful cooking volume—enough to handle chicken thighs, frozen fries, and vegetables in a single batch without the cramped feeling of smaller models. This size actually matters for family meals or meal prepping.
  • ✓ App control and WiFi connectivity work as advertised for most owners; you can genuinely start preheating from another room, which beats walking back to the kitchen mid-task. The interface isn't confusing, though it does require initial setup patience.
  • ✓ 4.3-star rating across 500+ reviews indicates consistent performance rather than being a lottery. Most buyers reporting issues cite app connectivity hiccups, not broken heating elements or failed units.

Cons
  • ✗ WiFi connectivity isn't bulletproof—several reviewers mention disconnects or sluggish app responsiveness, which defeats the purpose of smart features if you can't rely on them. This is the exact complaint that pops up repeatedly across the 500+ reviews.

Our Verdict

The Cosori Smart WiFi Air Fryer Oven 5.8L is genuinely competent at its core job—heating food quickly and crisping it effectively. The 5.8L capacity and app connectivity appeal to people who actually value remote preheating and scheduling. However, the app reliability issues mentioned across multiple reviews are real concerns, not isolated complaints. If you're buying primarily for the WiFi features and app control, acknowledge that you're betting on consistent connectivity; if those are secondary bonuses and you just want a large-capacity air fryer, there are simpler, cheaper alternatives that deliver identical cooking results. Frequently Asked Questions

Does the WiFi really work reliably, or is it a gimmick?

Mixed results based on the 500+ reviews. Most users report it works fine once set up, but several mention periodic disconnects requiring app reinstallation or router resets. It's functional but not rock-solid. If you're buying this unit expecting seamless remote control every single time, you might be disappointed. Treat the WiFi as a convenience feature that works 80% of the time, not 100%.

Can it actually fit a whole chicken or is 5.8L marketing hype?

Yes, it fits a whole chicken comfortably, though you'll want one under 4.5 lbs for optimal air circulation. The 5.8L capacity is legitimately larger than most compact models. The real question is whether the basket arrangement leaves awkward dead spaces—some reviewers note uneven cooking on bulkier items, so rotation mid-cooking might be necessary.

Is this better than a regular Cosori air fryer without WiFi?

Functionally? The heating and cooking results are virtually identical to non-smart Cosori models. The main difference is the app control and digital display. If smart features mean nothing to you and you want to save money, a standard Cosori air fryer delivers the same food quality. The WiFi version costs extra for convenience, not cooking performance.
