Frequently Asked Questions

How many cups does the Melitta carafe hold?

The standard Melitta pour over comes with a 10-ounce carafe, which brews roughly 1-2 cups of coffee depending on your cup size and strength preference. It's designed for single-serve or light duo brewing. If you're making coffee for four people regularly, this isn't your tool—you'd want a larger batch brewer.

What kind of filters do I need?

Melitta uses size #2 cone filters, which are widely available and cheap—often under $3 for a pack of 40. They're sold at every grocery store, online, and in bulk. Some people use metal filters with these drippers, though they're less common. Stick with paper unless you specifically want the slightly thicker mouthfeel metal filters create.

Does the 4.3-star rating mean most people keep using it?

The 40 reviews averaging 4.3 stars suggest solid satisfaction, though not universal love. Most of those stars likely come from people who understand what they're buying—a no-nonsense dripper. The people giving it 2-3 stars probably wanted more capacity, easier handling, or automatic features. Read the actual customer reviews to see if their complaints align with your pain points.

How does this compare to other budget drippers like Melitta's plastic version?

The ceramic version (this one) holds heat better and tastes slightly cleaner than plastic alternatives. Plastic is lighter and more durable if you travel, but ceramic makes noticeably better coffee in a side-by-side brew. Since you're only paying $10 versus $6-8 for plastic, the ceramic is worth it if you're brewing at home regularly.
