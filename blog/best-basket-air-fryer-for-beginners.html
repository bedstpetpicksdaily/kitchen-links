Removable, dishwasher-safe baskets are a game-changer because hand-washing a stuck basket three times a week will kill your enthusiasm faster than anything else.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What size air fryer basket do I need for a family of 4?

    A 4-6 quart basket air fryer is ideal for a family of 4, allowing you to cook enough food for a meal without overcrowding. If you frequently cook for more people or like meal prepping, consider a 6-8 quart model instead. Smaller baskets (2-3 quarts) work fine if it's just 1-2 people.

    Do basket air fryers cook food evenly without shaking the basket?

    Most basket air fryers require shaking the basket halfway through cooking for even results, though some newer models have rotating baskets that do this automatically. Even without shaking, results are generally even enough for beginners—just expect slightly better browning on one side if you skip it. If convenience is a priority, splurge on a model with automatic rotation.

    Is a basket or oven-style air fryer better for beginners?

    Basket air fryers are better for beginners because they're more affordable, take up less counter space, and have simpler controls. Oven-style models cook more food at once but are bulkier and pricier, making them better for larger households or experienced cooks.

    How much should I spend on a beginner-friendly air fryer?

    Budget $60-$120 for a quality beginner basket air fryer with good reliability and features—brands like Cosori, Instant Vortex, and Dreo offer solid options in this range. Avoid the cheapest models under $40 as they often have reliability issues, but you don't need to spend $200+ when starting out.
