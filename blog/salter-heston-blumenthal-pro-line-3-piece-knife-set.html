Do these need hand-washing, or can they go in the dishwasher?

Hand-wash only. Dishwashers are blade-dulling, handle-loosening machines disguised as time-savers. Yes, stainless steel resists corrosion in a dishwasher, but the heat cycling and mineral deposits will dull the edge faster than regular use. Wash by hand under warm water with mild soap, dry immediately, and you'll get years of sharp performance.

What's included in the set, and is a storage block worth buying separately?

You get three knives: the 8-inch chef's knife (your primary workhorse), the 5-inch utility knife (better control than the chef's knife for detailed work), and the 3.5-inch paring knife (for anything requiring single-hand precision). A magnetic strip on the wall is superior to a block—it takes less space, looks better, and keeps blades sharp longer since they're not crowded together. Most bundled block versions are mediocre plastic; skip them.
