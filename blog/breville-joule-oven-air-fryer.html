Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Breville Joule actually cook faster than a regular toaster oven?

Yes, noticeably so. Preheating takes 4-5 minutes versus 8-10 for standard toaster ovens, and cooking times are 15-25% faster due to the Element IQ heating system. A sheet of roasted vegetables takes 18 minutes instead of 25. That compounds across a week of cooking.

Is the smartphone app necessary or just marketing?

Necessary for the full experience, but not essential. The app lets you monitor temperature, get notifications when food hits target doneness, and access recipes. Without it, you still have a fantastic convection oven—just without remote monitoring. It's genuinely useful if you're meal prepping while helping kids with homework.

How does it compare to the Ninja or Cosori air fryer ovens at lower price points?

The Breville costs 30-40% more, but it outperforms on heating consistency and build quality. Ninja and Cosori models are solid for basic air frying and toasting, but they have hot spots and uneven browning more often. The Breville's heating zones eliminate this problem. Pay the premium if you cook frequently and care about results; buy a Ninja if you want a cheap workhorse for occasional air frying.

Will this actually replace my regular oven for everyday cooking?

For 80-90% of your cooking, yes. It won't roast a whole turkey or bake six loaves of bread simultaneously, but weeknight chicken, vegetables, reheated pizza, roasted proteins, and meal prep? Absolutely. You'll use your regular oven maybe once a week instead of five times.
