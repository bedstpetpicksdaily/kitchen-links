Frequently Asked Questions

How long do Bee's Wrap products actually last?

Manufacturers claim up to one year of regular use with proper care. Actual lifespan depends on washing frequency, water temperature, and how aggressively you stretch the material. Hand-washing in cool water extends life; hot water or dishwashers will degrade the beeswax coating faster. Most users report the products remain functional beyond one year but gradually lose their moldability.

Can I use Bee's Wrap bowl covers or bread bags with hot foods?

No. Beeswax has a relatively low melting point, so hot foods will cause the coating to soften, lose its seal integrity, and potentially transfer beeswax onto the food itself. Let hot foods cool to room temperature or slightly above before covering with Bee's Wrap products.

Are Bee's Wrap products safe for storing raw meat?

Bee's Wrap does not recommend using its products for raw meat storage due to cross-contamination risks and the difficulty of sanitizing the material thoroughly after contact with raw proteins. Use these for cooked foods, produce, bread, and leftovers only. For raw meat, use dedicated containers or disposable wrap.

How do I clean Bee's Wrap bowl covers and bread bags?

Hand-wash immediately after use with cool or lukewarm water and mild dish soap. Avoid hot water, dishwashers, and abrasive scrubbing. Air-dry completely before storing. If the coating becomes sticky or loses its grip, refresh it by placing in direct sunlight for a few hours, which will harden the beeswax slightly.

What if I have a bee allergy?

Bee's Wrap products contain beeswax, so they're not suitable if you have a documented beeswax allergy. However, most bee allergies are reactions to bee proteins in pollen or venom, not beeswax itself. Consult an allergist before use if you're uncertain, but beeswax allergies are rare. For true bee-free alternatives, look for plant-based wax wraps using candelilla or soy wax instead.
