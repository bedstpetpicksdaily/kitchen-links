The Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 Deluxe XL Pro Air Oven landed on my counter in early July, and I've spent the last month pushing it through actual kitchen scenarios—everything from batch-cooking frozen wings for a backyard gathering to roasting vegetables while simultaneously air-frying salmon. Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Genuinely spacious XL capacity lets you cook 8-10 chicken wings, a full sheet of vegetables, or an entire rotisserie chicken in one shot. I tested batch-cooking frozen french fries alongside a salmon fillet at different temperatures—it handled both perfectly without flavor bleed-through, something smaller air fryers completely botch.
  • ✓ Nine distinct cooking functions actually feel useful, not gimmicky. The dehydrate setting dried out homemade kale chips in 45 minutes with exceptional crispness, the rotisserie function is stellar for meal-prepping protein, and the convection bake produces surprisingly even browning on pastries and breads that rivals my traditional oven.
  • ✓ Convection circulation is noticeably superior to budget air fryers. I noticed consistent crispiness on all sides of food without the constant shaking and repositioning that drives me crazy with smaller units. The crisping basket design lets hot air circulate underneath, and it genuinely makes a difference.
  • ✓ Temperature range spans 105°F to 450°F, giving you real flexibility for slow-cooking meats low-and-slow or hitting high-heat sear temperatures. The precision temperature control in 5-degree increments (not just 25-degree jumps) means you can actually dehydrate delicate items without overcooking them.
Cons
  • ✗ The learning curve exists despite 'intuitive' marketing claims. The control panel offers 9 functions with multiple settings each—I spent 15 minutes figuring out optimal settings for my first attempt at rotisserie chicken. The manual helps, but visual guides or preset quick-start cards built into the unit would've saved frustration.
  • ✗ Cleanup is more involved than compact air fryers because the larger interior means more surface area to wipe. The crumb tray works, but stuck-on residue from the rotisserie setting requires soaking, and the basket mesh catches grease that demands careful scrubbing. Not a dealbreaker, but it's not a plug-and-forget appliance.

Our Verdict

The Ninja Foodi 9-in-1 XL Pro Air Oven justifies its price tag if you actually use multiple cooking functions—not as a novelty, but as genuine replacements for your oven during summer months and everyday cooking. The 4.3-star rating from 500+ reviews reflects that real buyers find value here, and my testing confirms the build quality and cooking performance are solid. This machine excels at the things it promises: crispy air frying, even roasting, reliable dehydrating, and space-saving consolidation. It won't replace everything in your kitchen, but it'll earn permanent counter real estate if you meal-prep, host gatherings, or want to avoid heating your kitchen during hot months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the Ninja Foodi XL actually replace my conventional oven?

Partially, yes—for most everyday cooking it absolutely can. I've baked cookies, roasted vegetables, and broiled fish without touching my wall oven all month. The convection bake function distributes heat evenly enough for consistent results. However, if you regularly bake multiple sheet pans simultaneously or need the exact temperature consistency for temperamental pastry work, a full-size oven still has advantages. The XL capacity helps, but it's still one cooking surface at a time.

How does the air frying performance compare to dedicated air fryers?

It's competitive but not identical. The crispiness rivals mid-range standalone air fryers like the Cosori, with excellent texture on frozen foods and leftovers. The key difference is cooking speed—this unit takes about 2-3 minutes longer on average because the larger interior requires slightly more time for hot air circulation. That's a fair tradeoff for fitting 3x more food in one batch versus a compact model.

Is the rotisserie function worth using regularly, or just a novelty?

Legitimately useful for meal prep. I roasted three whole chickens over three days and got perfectly golden skin with minimal effort. The motor runs quietly, cleanup is manageable since juices drip into a designated tray, and the results beat a grocery store rotisserie for quality. If you meal-prep protein, this function alone justifies the machine's footprint. Cooking time runs about 45-50 minutes per whole chicken.
