Yes, you can use metal utensils in an Instant Pot, but with important caveats. While stainless steel utensils won't damage the pot itself, they can scratch the non-stick coating on the inner pot, which is why manufacturers recommend using silicone, wood, or plastic utensils instead.

The Short Answer

Metal utensils won't harm your Instant Pot's functionality, but they will damage the non-stick inner pot coating over time. The Instant Pot's stainless steel exterior and heating elements can withstand metal without issue. However, the inner pot's non-stick surface is susceptible to scratches and peeling when exposed to metal utensils repeatedly. For this reason, Instant Pot officially recommends using soft-tipped utensils made from silicone, wood, or plastic.

The Full Explanation

Understanding why metal utensils are problematic requires looking at the Instant Pot's construction. The inner cooking pot features a non-stick coating designed to prevent food from sticking and make cleanup easier. This coating, typically made from materials like ceramic or Teflon-based compounds, is durable but not indestructible.

When you use metal utensils—such as stainless steel spoons, forks, or spatulas—to stir food or scrape the sides of the pot, you're creating microscopic scratches in this protective coating. While one or two uses might not cause visible damage, repeated use of metal utensils gradually deteriorates the non-stick surface. Once scratches penetrate the coating, food can stick to the exposed areas, and the coating may begin to peel or flake into your food.

The Instant Pot's exterior walls, heating elements, and lid are all made from durable stainless steel that can withstand metal contact without issues. The problem is exclusively with the inner pot's non-stick coating. This distinction is important because it means metal utensils aren't "dangerous" in the traditional sense—they won't electrocute you or cause the pot to malfunction. They simply accelerate wear and tear on a component designed for longevity.

What the Experts Say

Instant Pot's official guidelines explicitly state: "Use only non-metal utensils (wooden spoon, silicone spatula, etc.) when stirring or removing food from the inner pot."
