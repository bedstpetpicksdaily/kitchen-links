Can You Use Regular Pans in an Instant Pot?

No, you cannot use regular pans inside an Instant Pot during pressure cooking. The Instant Pot is designed to work with its included stainless steel inner pot or approved alternatives that can withstand high pressure and temperature without warping or compromising safety.

The Short Answer

Regular cookware is not safe to use inside an Instant Pot because standard pans aren't engineered to handle the intense pressure (up to 15 PSI) and heat that builds up during the cooking cycle. The Instant Pot's heating element, sealing ring, and pressure valve are calibrated specifically for the included pot or certified replacements. Using incompatible cookware risks damage to your appliance, poor cooking results, and potential safety hazards. Stick with stainless steel or ceramic pans that are explicitly designed for pressure cooking.

The Full Explanation

The Instant Pot operates under sealed pressure, which creates an environment completely different from traditional stovetop cooking. When you close the lid and lock it, internal pressure rises significantly, which is what allows food to cook faster and more efficiently. Regular pans—especially thin aluminum, non-stick, or glass cookware—simply aren't manufactured to withstand these conditions.

Why Regular Pans Don't Work:

  • Pressure Rating: Regular cookware has no pressure rating. It's designed for atmospheric cooking only, where pressure remains at sea level. Instant Pot pressures can reach 10.2 PSI (low) or 15 PSI (high), which can cause thin pans to warp, dent, or even fail.
  • Material Limitations: Non-stick coatings can degrade under high heat and pressure. Aluminum pans may react with acidic foods. Glass cookware can crack under thermal stress. These materials simply aren't compatible with the Instant Pot's sealed environment.
  • Fit and Seal Issues: The Instant Pot's sealing ring relies on a precise fit with the inner pot's rim. Regular pans won't create a proper seal, causing pressure to escape, preventing the cooker from building enough pressure to function correctly.
  • Heat Distribution: The Instant Pot's heating element is positioned to work with its specific pot design. Using a different pan can result in uneven heating, hot spots, or inadequate heat transfer, leading to undercooked food.

Safety Concerns:

Beyond performance issues, using incompatible cookware inside an Instant Pot creates legitimate safety risks. If pressure fails to build properly due to a poor seal, the appliance may malfunction. If a thin pan warps and breaks the seal mid-cooking, you could experience a sudden pressure release. In extreme cases, defective cookware could damage the appliance's internal components or heating element.

What the Experts Say

Instant Pot's official guidelines are clear: only use the included stainless steel inner pot or approved replacement pots. Instant Pot engineers have tested and certified specific cookware to ensure it meets their safety and performance standards. Consumer Reports and kitchen appliance experts consistently recommend against improvising with regular cookware, emphasizing that the Instant Pot's entire system—from the heating element to the pressure valve—is designed around the specifications of approved pots.

Professional food bloggers and Instant Pot specialists note that using an incorrect pot is one of the most common reasons people experience cooking failures, such as the "burn" notice appearing, food cooking unevenly, or the pot failing to reach pressure. The investment in proper cookware pays off in reliable, consistent results.
