Is the dual heat system worth the extra cost over a regular air fryer?

Yes, if you cook meat regularly. The dual heating genuinely reduces cooking time and improves texture. For someone cooking chicken breasts 3+ times per week, the time savings add up. If you mainly air fry frozen fries and vegetables, a standard air fryer does the job. But for real home cooks, the dual system justifies the premium.

It's genuinely useful as a secondary convection oven, especially during summer. You'll fit a 9x13 baking dish comfortably.
