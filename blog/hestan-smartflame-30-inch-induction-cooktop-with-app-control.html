Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use regular cookware on the Hestan Smartflame?

No. Induction cooktops only work with ferromagnetic cookware—cast iron, magnetic stainless steel, and some non-stick brands. Test with a magnet before buying: if it doesn't stick to the bottom, it won't heat. This is a dealbreaker if your kitchen is invested in copper or non-magnetic aluminum.

Is the app control worth the extra cost?

That depends entirely on your cooking habits. The app lets you adjust temperatures remotely and receive notifications, but it doesn't heat faster or produce better results than physical knob control. If you regularly prep multiple courses or run long simmers, remote monitoring offers convenience. For weeknight dinner cooking, it's novelty.

How does Smartflame compare to other premium induction options?

Compared to Miele or Thermador induction cooktops in the same price bracket, Smartflame prioritizes digital integration while delivering solid (but not exceptional) induction performance. You're paying a premium for the connected ecosystem. Traditional luxury brands emphasize build quality and heating precision—still excellent, but less 'smart.'

What's the real-world reliability like after a year?

The 4.3-star rating across 500+ reviews suggests solid durability, though some owners report app connectivity issues requiring firmware updates. Induction cooktops in general have fewer moving parts than gas, so mechanical failure is rare. Your risk is software glitches, not hardware breakdown.
