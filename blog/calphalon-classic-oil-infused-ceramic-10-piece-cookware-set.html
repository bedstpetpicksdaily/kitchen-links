Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the non-stick coating actually last?

Most reviewers report good non-stick performance for the first 18-24 months with normal use (hand-washing, medium heat, no metal utensils). After that, sticking increases noticeably. Some users extend life to 3 years by babying the pans, but this isn't a 'lifetime cookware' situation. The oil-infused ceramic helps initially but doesn't match the durability of traditional PTFE coatings.

Is this cookware oven-safe and at what temperature?

Yes, most Calphalon Classic pieces are oven-safe to 350°F, which covers braising, finishing dishes, and casserole-style cooking. Above 350°F and the handles become an issue—they transfer heat aggressively. This is a stovetop-primary set; don't expect high-heat oven versatility.

Does it work on induction cooktops?

No. This is not an induction-compatible set. The aluminum core doesn't have magnetic properties needed for induction cooking. If you have or plan to get an induction cooktop, this set won't work for you—you'll need ferrous-based cookware. Check your stove type before purchasing.

What's the real difference between 'oil-infused ceramic' and regular ceramic non-stick?

Oil-infused means the manufacturer blends vegetable oils into the ceramic coating during production to enhance initial slickness and heat distribution. It sounds premium but delivers modest practical benefits—your eggs will release slightly easier, but the coating still wears like regular ceramic. It's marketing-adjacent but not meaningless; there's genuine performance improvement in year one.

Is hand-washing necessary or can you use the dishwasher?

Technically dishwasher-safe according to specs, but hand-washing extends non-stick life significantly. Dishwasher heat and detergent aggressors accelerate coating degradation. If you hand-wash and dry immediately, you'll get 24+ months of solid performance. Dishwasher frequent users typically see decline by month 18.
