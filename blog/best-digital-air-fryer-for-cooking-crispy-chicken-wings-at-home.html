What to Look For

  • Basket capacity of 5.5+ quarts: Chicken wings need space to cook without crowding—overlapping wings steam rather than crisp. A larger basket ensures you can cook a full batch for 4-6 people in one go, and the air circulation around each wing is crucial for that crispy texture you're after.
  • Digital controls with preset functions: Look for air fryers with dedicated poultry settings or the ability to program exact temperatures (350-400°F) and times. Digital displays give you precise control over the cooking process, which is essential for wings that need consistent heat to render fat properly while keeping meat tender.
  • Rapid air circulation technology: The best results come from air fryers that distribute heat evenly around the entire basket. Check reviews specifically mentioning wing-cooking results, as some models have dead zones where wings cook unevenly—this is a dealbreaker for consistent crispiness.
