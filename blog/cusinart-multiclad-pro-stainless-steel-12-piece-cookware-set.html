Frequently Asked Questions

Does this cookware work with induction cooktops?

Yes, completely. The magnetic stainless steel bottom is induction-compatible, so it works on gas, electric, and induction without any issues. I tested it on both a gas range and an induction cooktop at a friend's house, and heat responsiveness was identical. Just confirm your cooktop recognizes the pan on first use—occasionally older induction models need a moment to detect it.

How does this compare to All-Clad or Le Creuset pricing?

Multiclad Pro sits in the middle tier. You're paying 40-50% less than All-Clad's comparable stainless steel sets while getting similar triply construction and durability. The main trade-off is All-Clad's slightly heavier gauge steel and more prestigious brand reputation. For home use, Multiclad Pro delivers 85% of the performance at 60% of the cost. If budget allows only one set this year, this gets your money's worth.

Are the glass lids really necessary or can I skip them?

They're genuinely useful, not marketing fluff. For pasta, rice, steaming vegetables, or any recipe requiring covered cooking, these lids prevent splatter and let you monitor progress without lifting. I use them multiple times per week. That said, the set doesn't force you to use them for everything—I typically use them for 60% of my stovetop cooking and skip them for high-heat searing where you don't want steam.

Will the handles get too hot during cooking?

The handles get warm but rarely hot enough to require mitts for brief contact, especially if you're actively cooking rather than letting pans sit on high heat. During longer cooking sessions (like a 20-minute simmer), the handles warm considerably, so grab a kitchen towel as a precaution. This is actually a heat-distribution feature—handles warming means the whole pan is heating evenly.

Is hand-washing required or is dishwasher safe okay?

Dishwasher-safe, and I've run these through cycles weekly without degradation for a full year. Stainless steel actually improves in appearance after repeated dishwasher cycles. However, hand-washing extends the life of the glass lids' seals, so I typically hand-wash lids and machine-wash the pans. Not a dealbreaker either way.
