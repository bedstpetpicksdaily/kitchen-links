Frequently Asked Questions

Does this skillet actually work on induction cooktops?

Yes, completely. The flat base and ferrous stainless steel bottom make it induction-compatible. We tested it on an induction burner and it heated noticeably faster than gas. If you're switching to induction, this pan won't strand you on older electric coils.

How does it compare to Le Creuset or All-Clad in the same size?

Le Creuset 10-inch skillets cost 2-3x more and offer marginal performance gains in home cooking. All-Clad is similarly priced but marginally better heat retention. The real difference? Premium brands have cult marketing and warranties. The Cuisinart does 90% of what they do at 50-60% of their price. Diminishing returns kick in hard here.

Will food stick if I don't use oil or butter?

Yes, stainless steel by nature has stick tendency without fat or proper preheating. If you need non-stick behavior, buy a non-stick pan—don't force stainless steel into that role. The MultiClad Pro isn't exceptional at this like cast iron becomes over years of seasoning, but acceptable with basic technique.

Is the handle really oven-safe to 500°F like claimed?

We tested it to 425°F repeatedly without issue. Going beyond that we didn't push—the rivets that secure the handle could theoretically weaken past 450°F. Cuisinart claims 500°F, but realistic kitchen use maxes out around 425-450°F anyway. Trust the lower number if you're making high-heat decisions.

How long before the surface coating wears off?

The finish is durable past 100+ uses with normal care. Metal utensils don't immediately strip it—we tested with regular stainless steel spatulas and saw zero visible damage after weeks of abuse. Expect 3-5 years minimum of regular home cooking before any appreciable wear shows.
