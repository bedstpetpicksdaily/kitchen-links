    Frequently Asked Questions

    What size air fryer is best for meal prepping?

    A 5-6 quart air fryer is ideal for meal prepping since it gives you enough capacity to cook larger batches without overcrowding, which ensures even cooking. Anything smaller than 5 quarts will require multiple cooking rounds, defeating the time-saving purpose of batch cooking. Look for models with a square or rectangular basket rather than round, as they maximize usable cooking space.

    Can you batch cook multiple foods at once in an air fryer?

    Yes, you can batch cook multiple foods simultaneously if they have similar cooking temperatures and times using air fryer dividers or by strategically placing items on different rack levels. However, you need to ensure foods don't overlap or block air circulation, as this causes uneven cooking. It's often more efficient to cook similar items together (like all proteins or all vegetables) rather than mixing different food types.

    How much money can you save by meal prepping with an air fryer?

    You can save 30-50% on groceries by meal prepping with an air fryer since batch cooking uses less energy per portion than cooking daily, and buying ingredients in bulk is cheaper than convenience foods. Air fryers also reduce food waste by allowing you to cook larger quantities and store portions throughout the week. The initial investment typically pays for itself within 2-3 months through reduced takeout and grocery costs.

    Is a budget air fryer worth it or should I spend more?

    Budget air fryers ($50-80) work just as well as expensive models for meal prepping—the main differences are build quality and preset programs, not actual cooking performance. For batch cooking, you really only need consistent temperature control and adequate capacity, both of which budget models provide. Spend more only if you want extra features like smart controls or larger capacity; otherwise, a budget model will serve you well.
