What to Look For

  • Larger capacity (5.8+ quarts): Batch cooking means cooking for multiple servings at once. A cramped basket forces you to work in batches within batches, wasting time and energy. Look for an air fryer with at least 5.8 quarts of usable space—ideally a square or rectangular design rather than round, since these maximize cooking surface for sheet pan-style meal prep.
  • Even heat distribution and reliable temperature control: Meal prep demands consistency. You need to know that a batch of chicken thighs cooks the same way on Monday as it does on Wednesday. Choose an air fryer with dual heating elements or proven even-cooking technology rather than the cheapest single-element model, which often creates hot spots and undercooked centers.
  • Dishwasher-safe, removable parts: When you're prepping five containers of food at once, cleanup matters. Opt for models with dishwasher-safe baskets and trays. This saves you 15-20 minutes per cooking session—time that adds up quickly when you're batch cooking regularly.
