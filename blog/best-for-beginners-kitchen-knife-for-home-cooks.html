What to Avoid

  • Avoid ultra-cheap knife sets: Beginner cooks often think they need a 12-piece set with a block, but cheap knives are dull out of the box and stay dull. Frequently Asked Questions

What size kitchen knife should a beginner buy?

An 8-inch chef's knife is the ideal size for beginners because it's versatile enough to handle most cutting tasks from chopping vegetables to slicing meat. It's large enough to be efficient but not so big that it's difficult to control, and one good 8-inch knife can replace multiple smaller knives in your kitchen.

Should I buy a stainless steel or carbon steel knife as a beginner?

Stainless steel is the better choice for beginners because it's more forgiving—it resists rust and staining even with inconsistent care and storage. Carbon steel knives are sharper but require regular maintenance and proper drying, making them better suited for experienced cooks.

How much should I spend on a beginner kitchen knife?

A quality beginner knife should cost between $30-$75, which gives you a durable, sharp blade without overspending on features you won't use yet. Avoid ultra-cheap knives under $15 as they won't hold an edge and are frustrating to use.

Do I need to sharpen my kitchen knife regularly?

Yes, a beginner should hone their knife every 2-4 weeks with a honing steel to maintain the edge, and sharpen it 1-2 times per year depending on use. A sharp knife is safer and makes cooking easier, so investing in a simple sharpening tool or taking it to a professional is worth it.
