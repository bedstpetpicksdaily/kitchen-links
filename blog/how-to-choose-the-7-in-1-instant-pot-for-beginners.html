What to Look For

  • Wattage between 900-1000W: Beginner-friendly 7-in-1 models typically operate in this range, providing consistent heating and cooking times. Higher wattage models cook faster but can be intimidating for new users who are still learning pressure cooking basics. This mid-range wattage ensures reliable performance without overwhelming complexity.
  • Stainless steel inner pot with clear measurement markings: A quality stainless steel pot (not non-stick) is more durable for beginners who are still learning proper cooking techniques, and clear fill line indicators help you avoid overfilling—a common beginner mistake. The markings make recipes easier to follow without constantly checking a manual.
  • Simple control panel with preset buttons: Look for models with one-touch buttons for common functions like "Soup," "Meat," and "Rice" rather than complex digital interfaces. These presets take the guesswork out of cooking times and pressure levels, allowing you to build confidence before experimenting with manual settings.
