What to Look For

1. Capacity and Size

Cold brew pitchers range from 32 ounces to 64+ ounces. Consider your household size and brewing frequency. A 32-ounce pitcher is perfect for singles or couples, while families should opt for 48-64 ounces. Remember that cold brew concentrate is diluted before serving, so a larger pitcher means fewer brewing cycles per week.

Standard cold brew takes 12-24 hours to extract properly. Look for pitchers with fine mesh filters or specially designed strainers that prevent grounds from ending up in your final product. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does cold brew coffee last in the pitcher?

Cold brew concentrate can last up to 2 weeks when stored in an airtight pitcher in the refrigerator. Once diluted with water or milk, it's best consumed within 24-48 hours for optimal flavor. Always check for any off smells or signs of mold before drinking.

What size cold brew pitcher do I need for my household?

A 32-48 oz pitcher works well for 1-2 people drinking daily, while a 64 oz pitcher is ideal for families or those who drink multiple cups. Consider your fridge space and how often you want to brew—larger pitchers mean less frequent brewing but take up more room.

Do I need a special pitcher or can I use any jar?

You can use any glass jar or container with a tight-fitting lid, but a dedicated cold brew pitcher has built-in filters or strainers that make straining easier and cleaner. Specialty pitchers also typically have measurement marks and spout designs that prevent drips, making them more convenient than repurposing regular jars.

How much ground coffee should I use in my cold brew pitcher?

Use a 1:4 or 1:5 ratio of coffee to water depending on how strong you prefer it—so for a 32 oz pitcher, use 6-8 oz of coarsely ground coffee. Start with 1:4 for bold concentrate and adjust down to 1:5 if you prefer a milder flavor.
