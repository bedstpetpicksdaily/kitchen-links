The Short Answer

Air fryers cook chicken remarkably fast compared to traditional ovens. Boneless, skinless chicken breasts usually need 12-15 minutes at 380°F, while bone-in thighs or drumsticks require 15-20 minutes at the same temperature. The exact time depends on thickness, whether the chicken is frozen, and your specific air fryer model. Always use a meat thermometer to verify the internal temperature has reached 165°F before serving.

The Full Explanation

Air fryer cooking times vary significantly based on several factors. Understanding these variables helps you achieve perfectly cooked chicken every time.

Chicken Breasts (Boneless, Skinless): These are the quickest to cook. A standard 6-8 oz breast takes 12-15 minutes at 380°F. For thicker breasts over 1 inch, add 2-3 minutes. Pound them to even thickness for more consistent cooking.

Chicken Thighs and Drumsticks: Bone-in pieces take longer due to the bone conducting heat differently. Plan for 15-20 minutes at 360°F. Skin-on thighs cook beautifully in air fryers, developing a crispy exterior in about 18 minutes.

Whole Chicken or Large Cuts: A small whole chicken (3-4 lbs) requires 30-35 minutes at 360°F. Larger birds may need 40-45 minutes. Flip halfway through for even browning.

Frozen Chicken: Cooking from frozen adds 5-8 minutes to cooking times. A frozen breast takes approximately 18-22 minutes at 380°F. Some cooks prefer thawing first for more even cooking.

Temperature Variations: Higher temperatures (400°F) cook faster but may brown the exterior before the inside is done. Lower temperatures (360°F) take slightly longer but offer more control. Find the sweet spot for your model through experimentation.

What the Experts Say

Professional chefs and appliance manufacturers consistently recommend checking internal temperature rather than relying solely on time. According to the USDA and most culinary experts, all poultry must reach an internal temperature of 165°F at the thickest part (usually the innermost thigh for whole birds or the thickest part of breasts). This is the only reliable way to ensure food safety. Many experienced air fryer users also note that preheating your air fryer for 3-5 minutes and arranging chicken in a single layer without overlap significantly improves cooking consistency and reduces cooking time variability.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long does it take to cook chicken breast in an air fryer?

    Boneless, skinless chicken breasts typically cook in 12-15 minutes at 380°F, depending on thickness. For thicker breasts (over 1 inch), check that the internal temperature reaches 165°F with a meat thermometer before serving.

    How long do chicken thighs take in an air fryer?

    Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs need about 20-25 minutes at 380°F, while boneless thighs cook faster in 15-18 minutes. Thighs are more forgiving than breasts and stay juicier even if slightly overcooked.

    Can you cook a whole chicken in an air fryer and how long?

    A whole chicken (4-5 pounds) takes approximately 50-60 minutes at 360°F in a standard air fryer, though cooking time varies by fryer size and chicken weight. Always check that the thickest part of the thigh reaches 165°F internally.

    How long does it take to cook frozen chicken in an air fryer?

    Frozen chicken breasts take about 20-25 minutes at 380°F without thawing first, adding roughly 5-10 minutes compared to thawed chicken. Frozen thighs or drumsticks may need 25-30 minutes depending on size.
