Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use an immersion blender to puree hot soup?

Yes, immersion blenders are designed to blend hot liquids directly in the pot. Always leave at least 2-3 inches of space at the top of your pot to prevent splashing, and let very hot soup cool for a minute or two before blending to minimize steam buildup and burns.

What wattage immersion blender do I need for soup?

A 400-500 watt immersion blender is sufficient for most home soups, though 600+ watts will blend tougher ingredients like nuts and beans faster and more smoothly. Higher wattage reduces blending time and creates a silkier texture, but isn't necessary for basic vegetable or cream soups.

Is a cordless immersion blender good enough for soup?

Cordless immersion blenders work for occasional soup blending but typically have less power and shorter battery life than corded models. If you make soup frequently, a corded blender is more reliable and consistent, though cordless is convenient for lighter blending tasks.

What's the difference between immersion blender brands for soup?

Premium brands like Braun and Bamix offer superior motor power and durability for frequent use, while budget brands like Hamilton Beach provide good value for occasional cooking. The key differences are motor strength, blade sharpness, and warranty—invest in a higher-end model if you plan to blend soup multiple times per week.
