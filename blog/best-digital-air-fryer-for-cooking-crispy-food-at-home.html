What to Avoid

  • Manual Dial Controls on Cheaper Models: Analog dials are imprecise and wear out quickly, leaving you guessing whether you're at 375°F or 400°F. This inconsistency is the enemy of crispy cooking. Digital controls cost a bit more but deliver the accuracy that separates mediocre results from excellent ones.
  • Basket Designs That Trap Moisture: Solid baskets without adequate holes or perforations create steam pockets that make food soggy instead of crispy. Always check product images to verify the basket has multiple drainage holes and an open design that allows air circulation from below.
