Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 6-quart capacity compare to the standard Instant Pot Duo?

The standard Duo is 5.7 quarts. In practical terms, that extra 0.3 quarts means you can comfortably cook for 8-10 people instead of 6-8. The real difference appears when batch cooking—you'll fit a full 3-4 pound pot roast with substantial vegetables without crowding, versus the standard model where you'd need to remove some liquid or vegetables. For solo cooks or couples, this difference is negligible.

Is the stainless steel inner pot worth the premium over the non-stick versions?

Yes, especially long-term. Non-stick coatings degrade with pressure and high heat. After 50+ cycles, scratches appear and food sticks differently. The stainless steel pot in this model shows virtually no degradation after 100+ uses. You're paying $50-75 more upfront but avoiding a replacement pot purchase in 2-3 years. That's genuine value.

Can you use this for canning or pressure canning like a traditional pressure canner?

No. The Instant Pot Duo Plus isn't approved for pressure canning by the USDA, even though it reaches the required PSI. You need a dedicated pressure canner (around $60-80) for safe canning. Don't try to use this as a replacement—it's designed for cooking, not preservation. This matters if canning is part of your kitchen plan.

How loud is it compared to stovetop pressure cookers?

Much quieter. The steam release is controlled and muffled through the valve system, unlike the hissing roar of traditional cookers. That said, the beeping and alerts are audible—some users find the notification sounds annoying during cooking. It's not silent, but it's neighbor-friendly.

What's the actual cooking time savings compared to slow cooking or oven methods?

Genuine: 45-minute pot roasts instead of 3+ hours in an oven, 15-minute dried beans instead of overnight soaking plus 2 hours simmering, 8-minute chicken breast instead of 20+ minutes. The tradeoff is preheating time (5-7 minutes) that oven methods don't require. For weeknight dinners, you're saving 60-80% of traditional cooking time. For weekend projects, the advantage shrinks.
