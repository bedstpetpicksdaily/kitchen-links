What to Look For

  • Capacity That Matches Your Household Size: Instant Pots come in 3-quart, 6-quart, and 8-quart sizes. A 6-quart is ideal for families of 4-6 or anyone who meal preps, while a 3-quart works perfectly for couples or small households. Don't assume bigger is always better—oversized cookers take longer to reach pressure and use more counter space.
  • Preset Programs That Align With Your Cooking Habits: Some models offer 7 preset programs while others have 15+. If you primarily cook rice, beans, and soups, a basic model suffices. However, if you want to pressure cook meats, steam vegetables, and make yogurt, look for models with more specialized functions like sous vide or sterilization settings.
  • Stainless Steel vs. Non-Stick Inner Pot: Stainless steel pots are durable, affordable, and work with acidic ingredients without degradation. Non-stick pots require gentler care but prevent sticking for delicate foods. Consider your cooking preferences and how much maintenance you're willing to do.
