Understanding Outdoor Griddles

An outdoor griddle features a large, flat cooking surface made from steel or cast iron. This design allows for even heat distribution across the entire cooking area, making it ideal for cooking multiple items simultaneously. Griddles excel at cooking foods that require a flat surface, such as pancakes, eggs, vegetables, and smaller cuts of meat. The flat surface also prevents food from falling through grates, which is especially useful when cooking delicate items or foods with marinades.

Big Horn Outdoors offers high-quality griddles that combine durability with superior heat retention. Their griddles are engineered with heavy-duty construction and precision temperature controls, ensuring consistent cooking results every time you fire them up.

Exploring Gas Grills

Gas grills use propane or natural gas as fuel and feature grates or bars for cooking. They're excellent for traditional grilling methods like searing steaks, grilling chicken, and cooking burgers. The open flame and grates allow for better smoke flavor and those coveted grill marks that many outdoor cooking enthusiasts love. Gas grills typically heat up quickly and offer excellent temperature control through adjustable burners.

Big Horn Outdoors gas grills are engineered for performance, featuring multiple burners, ample cooking space, and weather-resistant materials that withstand the elements. Their grills are built to last and designed for both casual family cookouts and entertaining larger groups.

Key Cooking Differences

The cooking experience differs significantly between these two options. Griddles provide consistent, even heat across the entire surface, making them perfect for batch cooking breakfast foods or stir-frying vegetables. Gas grills, meanwhile, create distinct heat zones—hotter areas near the burners and cooler zones to the sides—which is excellent for managing different cooking temperatures simultaneously. If you plan to cook a variety of foods or prefer traditional grilling with char marks and smoke flavor, a gas grill might be your preference. However, if you enjoy cooking breakfast-style meals, Asian cuisine, or need to cook for large groups efficiently, a griddle from Big Horn Outdoors could be the superior choice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use a griddle and gas grill together?

Yes, many outdoor cooking enthusiasts use both pieces of equipment. Some even opt for combination units that feature both griddle and grill surfaces. Big Horn Outdoors offers versatile options that allow you to expand your cooking capabilities.

Which cooks food faster, a griddle or gas grill?

Gas grills typically preheat faster due to their burner design, reaching cooking temperature in 5-10 minutes. However, griddles maintain consistent heat across the entire surface, which can speed up batch cooking times for items like pancakes or vegetables.

Are outdoor griddles easy to clean?

Yes, griddles are generally easier to clean than gas grills since the flat surface allows you to scrape food debris directly into a drip tray. Most Big Horn Outdoors griddles feature smooth surfaces and convenient grease management systems.

What's the price difference between griddles and gas grills?

Prices vary widely based on size and features. Quality griddles and gas grills from Big Horn Outdoors start at similar price points, with premium models offering additional features like side burners, temperature gauges, and storage space.

Can griddles be used for camping?

Yes, portable camping griddles and stoves from Big Horn Outdoors are perfect for outdoor adventures. They're compact, lightweight, and deliver the same cooking performance as larger models.
