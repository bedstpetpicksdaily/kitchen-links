Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 10-quart size actually cook faster than smaller models?

Not per portion—pressure times remain the same. The advantage is cooking multiple portions simultaneously. I prepared 24 servings of pulled chicken in one cycle versus three cycles in a 6-quart. The time savings compound when you batch cook regularly.

Is this better than buying two 6-quart Instant Pots?

Depends on counter space and your cooking style. One 10-quart takes less counter real estate than two 6-quart models, but you lose flexibility (you can't cook two different meals simultaneously). For batch cooking identical recipes, the 10-quart wins.

How does it perform with the slow cooker setting for summer entertaining?

Excellent. I used it to keep a full batch of pulled pork at serving temperature for 6 hours during a July gathering. The temperature stayed consistent, and the meat didn't dry out or overcook like it can in smaller slow cookers where the heating element concentrates more intensely.
