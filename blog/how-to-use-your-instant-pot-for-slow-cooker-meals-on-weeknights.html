What to Look For

  • Slow Cook Function with Low and High Settings – Make sure your Instant Pot has dedicated slow cooker modes that reach temperatures between 190°F (low) and 280°F (high). Not all models have this feature, so verify before purchasing if slow cooking is your priority.
  • Adequate Capacity for Family Meals – A 6-quart or 8-quart Instant Pot gives you enough room to cook proteins and vegetables that feed 4+ people without overcrowding, which is essential for even cooking on slow cook cycles.
  • Reliable Keep Warm Function – Look for models with an automatic keep warm setting that maintains your food at a safe temperature for extended periods. This is crucial when you're running late and need dinner to stay warm for an extra hour or two.

Why This Works for This Situation

Your Instant Pot's slow cooker function removes the biggest barrier to weeknight cooking: time. You can brown your meat and sauté aromatics the night before (or during your morning coffee), dump everything in the pot with stock and seasonings, set it to low for 6-8 hours, and walk away. Unlike a traditional slow cooker that takes up permanent counter real estate, your Instant Pot transforms into a pressure cooker for those nights when you need dinner in 20 minutes instead. This dual functionality means one appliance handles your entire cooking strategy, which is a game-changer for small kitchens.

The psychological advantage matters too—knowing dinner is cooking during your workday eliminates the 5 p.m. panic of "what's for dinner?" You'll feel more relaxed coming home, and your family gets hot, homemade meals even on your busiest weeks. Plus, slow cooked dishes in an Instant Pot develop deeper, more complex flavors than quick pressure cooking alone, so you're not sacrificing quality for convenience.

What to Avoid

  • Don't Overfill the Pot for Slow Cooking – While Instant Pots can hold a lot, filling beyond the two-thirds line for slow cooking can result in uneven cooking and potential overflow. Leave headroom so steam can circulate properly.
  • Avoid Using High Temperatures for Delicate Proteins – Slow cook on high is too aggressive for fish, shrimp, or tender cuts; save high for beef stew and tough cuts that benefit from aggressive heat. Low and slow is almost always the better choice for weeknight meals.
  • Don't Skip the Sauté Step for Depth of Flavor – While you technically can throw raw ingredients directly into slow cook mode, you'll miss the caramelization that makes weeknight meals taste restaurant-quality. Spend 5 minutes browning meat first—it makes a real difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you really use an Instant Pot as a slow cooker?

Yes, most Instant Pot models have a slow cooker function built in, allowing you to cook meals on low or high heat for extended periods just like a traditional slow cooker. This makes it a versatile kitchen tool that can handle both pressure cooking and slow cooking without needing a separate appliance.

How long does food take to cook on Instant Pot slow cooker mode?

Instant Pot slow cooker mode typically takes 4-8 hours on low or 2-4 hours on high, similar to traditional slow cookers. Cooking times depend on the type and size of ingredients, so a chicken breast might cook in 4 hours on low while a tough cut of beef could need 8 hours.

Do you need to adjust slow cooker recipes for an Instant Pot?

Most traditional slow cooker recipes work directly in an Instant Pot with little to no adjustment needed. However, you may want to reduce liquids slightly since the Instant Pot doesn't lose as much moisture during cooking, and avoid adding delicate ingredients like dairy until the end.

Is it worth buying an Instant Pot just for slow cooking?

If you only need slow cooking, a traditional slow cooker is more affordable and may be better suited to your needs. However, if you want versatility to also pressure cook, sauté, make yogurt, or steam, an Instant Pot offers excellent value as a multi-functional kitchen workhorse.
