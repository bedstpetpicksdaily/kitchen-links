What to Look For

  • Slow Cook Function with Low and High Settings – Make sure your Instant Pot has dedicated slow cooker modes that reach temperatures between 190°F (low) and 280°F (high). Not all models have this feature, so verify before purchasing if slow cooking is your priority.
  • Adequate Capacity for Family Meals – A 6-quart or 8-quart Instant Pot gives you enough room to cook proteins and vegetables that feed 4+ people without overcrowding, which is essential for even cooking on slow cook cycles.
  • Reliable Keep Warm Function – Look for models with an automatic keep warm setting that maintains your food at a safe temperature for extended periods. This is crucial when you're running late and need dinner to stay warm for an extra hour or two.

What to Avoid

  • Don't Overfill the Pot for Slow Cooking – While Instant Pots can hold a lot, filling beyond the two-thirds line for slow cooking can result in uneven cooking and potential overflow. Leave headroom so steam can circulate properly.
  • Avoid Using High Temperatures for Delicate Proteins – Slow cook on high is too aggressive for fish, shrimp, or tender cuts; save high for beef stew and tough cuts that benefit from aggressive heat. Low and slow is almost always the better choice for weeknight meals.
  • Don't Skip the Sauté Step for Depth of Flavor – While you technically can throw raw ingredients directly into slow cook mode, you'll miss the caramelization that makes weeknight meals taste restaurant-quality. Spend 5 minutes browning meat first—it makes a real difference.
