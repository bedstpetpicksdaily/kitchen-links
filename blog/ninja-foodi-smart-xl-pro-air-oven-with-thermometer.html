Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Genuine versatility: air fries, bakes, roasts, dehydrates, and broils in one unit. If you're actually using 6+ cooking methods, consolidating appliances saves counter real estate that many kitchens desperately need.
  • ✓ Smart temperature probe thermometer included: You get accurate internal temperature readings without buying a separate meat thermometer ($20-40 elsewhere). This alone appeals to precision-minded cooks who've burned chicken thighs one too many times.
  • ✓ Large capacity XL sizing: Fits 9 slices of toast or a full rotisserie chicken, which matters if you're cooking for families or meal-prepping. Standard air fryers feel cramped by comparison.
  • ✓ Dual heating elements deliver faster preheat and more even cooking than single-element competitors. Real customers note crispier results on french fries and more consistent browning on baked goods—the 4.3-star rating reflects this consistency.
Cons
  • ✗ Price tag is genuinely steep. You're looking at $300+ for this unit, while solid air fryer alternatives from Cosori or Instant Pot cost $150-200 for similar core functionality. That's a significant premium for features you might never use.
  • ✗ Bulky footprint occupies serious counter space despite the 'compact' marketing language. The XL size means it won't fit in smaller kitchens or apartments—measure your space before buying, or you'll be returning it frustrated.

Our Verdict

The Ninja Foodi Smart XL Pro Air Oven justifies its price only if you genuinely plan to use 4+ of its cooking functions regularly. The included thermometer, dual heating elements, and capacity are legitimately useful features backed by real customer satisfaction. However, if you only air fry and occasionally bake, you're paying $100+ premium for features collecting dust. Budget-conscious shoppers should seriously consider a $150 dual-basket air fryer from Cosori plus a $30 meat thermometer instead—you'll save money and get better single-function performance. The 4.3-star rating is solid, but it reflects satisfied owners who actually utilize the versatility. Be honest with yourself about your cooking habits before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Ninja Foodi Smart XL Pro worth buying in 2026, or should I wait for sales?

July is actually decent timing for kitchen appliance sales—many retailers push summer deals before Black Friday. Check Amazon's price history; this model regularly drops $20-40 from MSRP during seasonal promotions. If you see it under $270, that's reasonable. Above $350? Wait for a sale or consider alternatives like the Instant Pot Pro Max or Cosori Smart Air Fryer, which deliver 80% of the functionality at 60% of the cost.

How does the thermometer feature compare to buying a separate meat thermometer?

The included smart thermometer is genuinely convenient—it wirelessly alerts you when your chicken reaches 165°F or steak hits your target temp. It's not revolutionary, but it eliminates the step of manually checking doneness. A standalone Meater Plus thermometer ($99) or basic ThermoPro ($25) do similar jobs. The difference: this thermometer integrates with the oven's app, so you get one unified interface. Worth the premium? Only if you cook meat frequently and value app notifications.

Will this actually replace my regular oven, or is it just hype?

It can replace your regular oven for 70-80% of tasks—roasting vegetables, baking small batches of cookies, air frying chicken wings. Where it struggles: baking large cakes, pizza nights for 8 people, or anything requiring precise convection timing. The XL size helps, but it's not a true oven replacement for serious bakers. Think of it as a primary appliance for singles and couples, a secondary appliance for families.
