Can You Put Metal in an Air Fryer?

Yes, you can put metal in an air fryer, but only certain types and with important restrictions. Metal cookware like aluminum foil, stainless steel pans, and metal skewers are safe to use in most air fryers, as long as they don't touch the heating element.

The Short Answer

Metal cookware is generally safe in air fryers because air fryers work by circulating hot air rather than using direct heating elements like microwaves do. The key is ensuring your metal doesn't come into contact with the heating coil and that you're using microwave-safe metal products. Most stainless steel pans, aluminum foil, and metal accessories designed for air fryers won't cause problems. However, certain metal items—like metal mesh baskets or anything that could obstruct airflow—should be avoided.

The Full Explanation

Understanding how air fryers work is essential to knowing what metal is safe. Air fryers use a heating element and a fan to circulate hot air at high speeds around your food. Unlike microwaves, which use electromagnetic radiation that reacts negatively with metal, air fryers simply blow hot air. This means metal won't cause arcing or electrical hazards.

Safe Metal Items in Air Fryers:

  • Aluminum foil: Completely safe and widely recommended for lining baskets, wrapping foods, or creating dividers. Many users wrap delicate items or foods prone to splattering.
  • Stainless steel pans and ramekins: Small pans, baking dishes, and ramekins fit perfectly in most air fryer baskets and cook evenly.
  • Metal skewers: Perfect for kebabs and grilled items, as long as they don't touch the heating element.
  • Metal grates and racks: Aftermarket metal accessories designed specifically for air fryers are safe to use.
  • Metal trivets: Help elevate food and improve air circulation.

Metal Items to Avoid:

  • Metal mesh baskets: These can restrict airflow and prevent proper cooking.
  • Anything touching the heating coil: The top heating element should never come into contact with metal to avoid potential damage to the appliance.
  • Aluminum containers with non-removable lids: Completely enclosed metal containers trap heat and prevent air circulation.
  • Thin, flimsy metal: Lightweight metal can warp, bend, or move around during the cooking process.

Best Practices: Always ensure there's at least half an inch of clearance between any metal item and the heating element. Leave space around metal cookware to allow hot air to circulate properly. If you're using aluminum foil, keep it smooth and secure so pieces don't blow around inside your air fryer. Check your specific air fryer's manual, as some manufacturers have particular recommendations.

What the Experts Say

Kitchen appliance experts consistently confirm that metal cookware is safe in air fryers because of the convection cooking method. Most air fryer manufacturers explicitly allow aluminum foil and stainless steel accessories in their instruction manuals. The consensus among culinary professionals is that metal actually enhances air frying by improving heat distribution and cooking consistency. Experts recommend investing in quality metal accessories designed for air fryers rather than improvising with random metal items from your kitchen.
