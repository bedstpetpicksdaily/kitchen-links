Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my Breville burrs actually need replacing?

Three clear signs: espresso shots extract noticeably faster (pulling in under 20 seconds for single shots), the grinder produces inconsistent particle sizes visible in the portafilter basket, or it makes grinding sounds that are distinctly different from when new. If you're seeing all three, burr replacement is overdue. Some users wait until extraction speed increases 5+ seconds—that's waiting too long.

Are these actual Breville parts or aftermarket replacements?

These are genuine OEM Breville components designed specifically for the Impress model. Verify when ordering—legitimate sellers will explicitly state 'Official Breville Replacement Part' or 'OEM.' Counterfeit burr sets do exist and perform poorly; they grind inconsistently and wear faster. Buy from authorized retailers or verified Amazon sellers with substantial positive history to avoid counterfeits.

Will installation void my warranty?

Replacing consumable parts like burrs typically doesn't void your warranty, but check your specific machine's documentation first. Breville generally considers grinder burrs maintenance items rather than warranty-voiding modifications. If your machine is still under coverage and you're uncertain, contact Breville support before opening the grinder—better to confirm than risk complications.

How long do replacement burrs actually last compared to original ones?

Genuine Breville replacement burrs last approximately as long as original equipment—roughly 12-18 months with daily use grinding espresso. Some reviewers report 18-24 months with lighter use (3-4 cups daily) versus heavier cafe-style volume grinding 20+ shots daily. The wear rate depends on how frequently you grind and grind fineness; espresso grind sizes are harder on burrs than coarser settings.
