Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ceramic coating really last longer than regular nonstick?

In my experience, yes—significantly. Traditional PTFE nonstick starts degrading around the six-month mark with regular use, especially if you ever use metal utensils or higher heat. This ceramic coating has maintained release properties at three months of weekly use, including occasional high-heat searing. That said, proper care (hand-washing, avoiding extreme temperature swings) absolutely matters. You can't abuse ceramic coating any more than you can traditional nonstick; it just handles normal wear better.

Is this cookware safe for induction cooktops?

No—Caraway's aluminum construction won't work with induction. If you have or plan to install an induction cooktop, this set isn't compatible. Check your current stovetop type before purchasing. This is a significant limitation worth knowing upfront, though it doesn't affect gas or electric coil users.

How does the oven-safe temperature limit affect everyday cooking?

The cookware is oven-safe to 350°F, which covers most home cooking scenarios but excludes high-heat broiling or professional baking. For roasting vegetables, finishing a skillet dinner, or baking, you're fine. If you frequently make dishes requiring 400°F+ oven temperatures, traditional stainless steel might suit you better. Realistically, most home cooks stay below 350°F, so this limitation matters less than it initially sounds.
