What to Look For

  • A yogurt-specific function: Look for an Instant Pot model with a dedicated "Yogurt" button or setting. This ensures you can maintain the exact 110°F temperature needed for cultures to thrive, rather than relying on manual temperature control.
  • Adequate capacity: Choose a 6-quart or larger Instant Pot if you want to make yogurt regularly. A larger capacity means you can batch-make enough yogurt to last a week, and there's more room for the heating process to work efficiently.
  • Clear inner pot markings: Select a model with visible measurement lines on the inner stainless steel pot. When making yogurt, you need to see exactly how much milk you're using to get the right consistency and know when fermentation is complete.

Why This Works for This Situation

The Instant Pot's sealed environment and precise temperature control eliminate the biggest challenge beginners face: keeping milk at a consistent 110°F during the 8-12 hour fermentation process. Without this precision, your yogurt cultures won't activate properly, leaving you with expensive milk instead of creamy yogurt. The Instant Pot does this automatically, meaning you can simply add milk and starter culture, set it, and walk away—no thermometers, no checking the temperature every hour, no stress.

Another reason this works so well is that the Instant Pot is a tool you likely already have or will use for many other meals. You're not buying a single-purpose yogurt maker; you're using an appliance that's already in your kitchen. For beginners especially, this removes the barrier to trying something new. Once you've mastered basic yogurt, you can experiment with flavored yogurts, Greek yogurt, and even non-dairy versions—all in the same machine.

What to Avoid

  • Don't skip pasteurizing your milk first: Some beginners think they can skip the boil step, but pasteurizing kills unwanted bacteria that could interfere with your starter culture. Always use the Yogurt Boil function before fermenting, or heat milk on the stovetop to 180°F and let it cool first.
  • Don't use ultra-old starter cultures: Yogurt starter loses potency over time. If your starter is more than 2 weeks old or you're unsure when you opened it, buy a fresh yogurt with active cultures from the store. This is one area where spending a couple dollars extra prevents wasting milk and time.
  • Don't rely on older Instant Pot models without a yogurt function: Early Instant Pot versions don't have consistent yogurt settings, making temperature control difficult for beginners. Stick with models from 2015 onward that explicitly feature a Yogurt button.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need special ingredients to make yogurt in an Instant Pot?

You only need two ingredients: milk and a yogurt starter (either store-bought plain yogurt with live cultures or yogurt powder). Whole milk, 2%, or even non-dairy milk like coconut or oat milk all work well. Make sure your starter contains live active cultures, which you can verify by checking the label.

What Instant Pot models can make yogurt?

Most Instant Pot models made after 2014 have a yogurt function, including the Duo, Duo Plus, Ultra, and Smart models. If you have an older model without a yogurt button, you can still make yogurt using the sous vide function or by manually controlling the temperature with the saute button.

How long does it take to make yogurt in an Instant Pot?

The actual cooking time is about 30 minutes to heat the milk, but the fermentation process takes 8-12 hours depending on how tangy you like it and your room temperature. Most people start the fermentation in the evening and have finished yogurt by morning.

Can I use Greek yogurt as a starter for homemade yogurt?

Yes, Greek yogurt works great as a starter since it contains live cultures, but use regular (non-Greek) yogurt if possible because Greek yogurt is strained and has less whey, which can affect consistency. If you only have Greek yogurt, use about 2-3 tablespoons per quart of milk instead of the usual 2 tablespoons.
