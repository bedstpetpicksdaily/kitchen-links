    Frequently Asked Questions

    What's the difference between an Instant Pot and a regular pressure cooker?

    Instant Pots are electric pressure cookers with multiple built-in programs (pressure cook, slow cook, sous vide, etc.), while traditional pressure cookers are stovetop-only and require manual temperature monitoring. Instant Pots offer more convenience and safety features like automatic pressure release, but traditional pressure cookers heat up faster and are typically more affordable.

    Is a 6 quart Instant Pot worth it for a family of 4?

    A 6-quart Instant Pot is ideal for families of 4+ or meal preppers, as it allows you to batch cook and store leftovers easily. For smaller households, a 6-quart may be overkill since you can't fill it more than two-thirds full, but it gives you flexibility for entertaining or large-batch recipes.

    Which 6 quart pressure cooker is most reliable and lasts longest?

    Instant Pot and Presto are the most trusted brands for durability; Instant Pots typically last 5-10 years with proper care, while Presto stovetop models can last 20+ years. Check customer reviews for the specific model, as failure rates vary—Instant Pot's Max and Pro models have better track records than budget versions.

    Can I use a 6 quart pressure cooker for canning?

    Most electric Instant Pots are NOT approved for pressure canning by the USDA, as they don't reach the required 15 PSI consistently. Traditional stovetop pressure cookers like Presto are specifically designed for canning and are USDA-approved; if canning is important to you, choose a stovetop model.
