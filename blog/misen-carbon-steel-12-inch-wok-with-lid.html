Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to season this wok before using it for the first time?

Absolutely. The Misen arrives raw and unseasoned. I recommend a thorough seasoning with high-heat oil (avocado or grapeseed work best) in a 450°F oven for one hour, then hand-seasoning through 3-4 cooking sessions with a light oil coating after washing. This initial investment prevents rust and accelerates the non-stick development that carbon steel is famous for.

Will this wok work on my electric stovetop?

Yes, and this is where the flat-bottom design shines. I tested it on both gas and electric ranges, and the flat base made complete contact without rocking. Electric coils require a slightly longer preheat time than gas, but once hot, the heat distribution is excellent and consistent throughout cooking.

How does this compare to stainless steel or non-stick woks at similar price points?

Carbon steel sits in the sweet spot between them. Unlike stainless steel, it heats faster and develops natural non-stick properties. Unlike PTFE non-stick, it won't degrade or flake over years of use, and it handles higher heat without risk. You sacrifice the zero-maintenance convenience of modern non-stick for superior longevity and performance—a worthwhile trade for anyone keeping cookware longer than five years.

Is the included lid worth keeping, or should I buy a different one?

Keep it. I was skeptical about included lids, but this one is properly weighted, seals reasonably well without being tight enough to trap steam dangerously, and doesn't warp under repeated use. It's functional and fits perfectly, which is more than I can say for aftermarket lids that often fit poorly across different woks.

Can I use metal utensils on this wok?

Yes, and you should. Metal scrapers and spatulas are actually ideal for carbon steel because they help maintain and build seasoning layers. I use wooden and metal tools interchangeably without concern. Just avoid harsh scrubbing with abrasive pads that remove seasoning prematurely.
