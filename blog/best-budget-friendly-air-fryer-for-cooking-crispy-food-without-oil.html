If you're craving crispy, delicious food without the guilt of deep frying, an air fryer is a game-changer—but the price tag can be intimidating. Finding a budget-friendly air fryer that actually delivers crispy results without breaking the bank requires knowing exactly what features matter and which ones are just marketing hype. What to Look For

  • Wattage between 1,200-1,400W: This power range is the sweet spot for budget models. It's enough to heat quickly and crisp food effectively without the premium price tag of high-wattage models. Lower wattage means slower cooking and potentially uneven results, so don't go below 1,200W even if the price is tempting.
  • Capacity of 3.5-4 quarts: This size is perfect for individuals and couples while staying affordable. It's large enough for weeknight dinners and small enough to heat efficiently without wasting energy on empty space. Skip the massive 6+ quart models if you're cooking for one or two—you'll pay for capacity you don't use.
  • Basket design with perforated holes: Look for baskets with adequate ventilation holes rather than solid surfaces. Better air circulation means crispier results with less oil needed, which is the whole point of air frying on a budget. Non-stick coating is helpful but less important than good airflow design.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the best cheap air fryer under $100?

    The Cosori Air Fryer Oven and Instant Vortex Plus are excellent budget options under $100 that deliver crispy results without sacrificing quality. Both models have 3.5-4 quart capacities, multiple cooking functions, and reliable heating elements that rival pricier brands. Look for sales during holidays—these often drop to $60-80.

    Do budget air fryers actually cook food crispy without oil?

    Yes, budget air fryers work just as well as expensive ones at creating crispy food because they all use the same rapid air circulation technology. The key is preheating for 3-5 minutes, arranging food in a single layer, and shaking the basket halfway through cooking—not the price tag. Even a $50 air fryer will produce crispy fries and chicken wings.

    How much capacity do I need in a budget air fryer?

    For a single person or couple, a 2-3 quart air fryer is sufficient; for families of 4+, choose 4-5 quarts to avoid multiple batches. Smaller models are cheaper ($40-60) but limit cooking one meal at once, while larger budget models ($80-100) offer better value for families. Measure your kitchen counter space first since even budget models take up 12-15 inches.

    Are budget air fryers worth buying or should I spend more?

    Budget air fryers ($50-100) are absolutely worth buying because the core heating technology is identical to $300 models—you're mainly paying for brand name and extra features like smart controls. Most home cooks never use advanced functions; a basic budget model with temperature control and a timer covers 95% of cooking needs. Save your money and invest in a quality air fryer cookbook instead.
