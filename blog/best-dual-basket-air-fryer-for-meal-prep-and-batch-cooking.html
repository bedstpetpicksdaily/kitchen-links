What to Look For

  • Independent basket controls: Look for models where each basket has its own temperature and timer settings. This is non-negotiable for meal prep, since you'll often need to cook chicken at 380°F while vegetables need 360°F, all finishing at different times. Single control systems force you to compromise on either doneness or texture.
  • Adequate capacity per basket: Aim for at least 5.8 quarts per basket (11+ quarts total). For batch cooking, you want each basket to hold a full sheet pan's worth of food, not require you to cook in multiple batches. Smaller baskets defeat the purpose of owning a dual model.
  • Even heat distribution and smart spacing: The baskets should be positioned to allow proper air circulation without one basket's heat affecting the other. Read reviews specifically mentioning cooking results when both baskets are running simultaneously—some cheaper models have significant hot spots or uneven browning when fully loaded.
