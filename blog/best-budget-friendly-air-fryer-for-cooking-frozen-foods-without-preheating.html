What to Look For

  • Fast heat-up capability with minimal preheating: Look for models with 1500-1700 watts of power that reach cooking temperature quickly. Some budget air fryers can go from cold to cooking in under 2-3 minutes, or skip preheating entirely for frozen foods with only 1-2 minute adjustments to cooking time.
  • Spacious basket with even air circulation: A basket capacity of at least 4-5 quarts ensures frozen portions cook evenly without needing to batch cook. Better air circulation means you won't need to shake or flip food halfway through, saving you time and attention.
  • Simple, reliable temperature control: Digital or mechanical controls that let you easily adjust from 300°F to 400°F give you flexibility for different frozen foods. Avoid overly complicated models—you want straightforward settings that work consistently without a learning curve.
