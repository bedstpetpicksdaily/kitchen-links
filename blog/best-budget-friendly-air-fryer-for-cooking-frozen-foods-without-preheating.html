What to Look For

  • Fast heat-up capability with minimal preheating: Look for models with 1500-1700 watts of power that reach cooking temperature quickly. Some budget air fryers can go from cold to cooking in under 2-3 minutes, or skip preheating entirely for frozen foods with only 1-2 minute adjustments to cooking time.
  • Spacious basket with even air circulation: A basket capacity of at least 4-5 quarts ensures frozen portions cook evenly without needing to batch cook. Better air circulation means you won't need to shake or flip food halfway through, saving you time and attention.
  • Simple, reliable temperature control: Digital or mechanical controls that let you easily adjust from 300°F to 400°F give you flexibility for different frozen foods. Avoid overly complicated models—you want straightforward settings that work consistently without a learning curve.

What to Avoid

  • Ultra-compact models under 2 quarts: These "personal" air fryers save counter space but force you to cook frozen foods in multiple batches. Since batch cooking multiplies your total time investment, the convenience advantage disappears—you're better served by a larger basket even if it takes up slightly more space.
  • Models requiring mandatory preheating: Some budget air fryers have heating elements that work best with preheating, negating your time-saving advantage. Read reviews specifically mentioning frozen food preparation; if users say "you must preheat," keep looking.
  • Unclear wattage or poorly designed baskets: Air fryers under 1200 watts heat slowly and may cook frozen foods unevenly. Similarly, baskets with poor ventilation or uneven heating holes mean some frozen items cook while others stay partially frozen—frustrating and unreliable for meal planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you cook frozen foods in an air fryer without preheating?

Yes, most air fryers can cook frozen foods without preheating, though you may need to add 3-5 minutes to the cooking time. Budget-friendly models work just as well as expensive ones for this purpose—simply place your frozen food in the basket and set your desired temperature. Just shake or flip items halfway through for even cooking.

What's the cheapest air fryer that actually works well for frozen foods?

Air fryers under $50-70 from brands like Cosori, Dash, or Instant Vortex work reliably for frozen foods without preheating. Look for models with at least 2.6-3 quart capacity and 1400+ watts of power, as these specs ensure consistent heating for frozen items without breaking your budget.

Do budget air fryers cook frozen fries and chicken wings evenly?

Budget air fryers cook frozen fries and wings evenly if you don't overcrowd the basket and shake them halfway through cooking. Cheaper models have the same heating element technology as expensive ones, so results are comparable—the main difference is build quality and durability, not cooking performance.

How much does a good budget air fryer cost for frozen food cooking?

A reliable budget air fryer for frozen foods typically costs $40-80, with the sweet spot around $60 for quality brands. At this price point, you'll get enough capacity and wattage to cook frozen meals efficiently without needing to spend $150+ on premium models.
