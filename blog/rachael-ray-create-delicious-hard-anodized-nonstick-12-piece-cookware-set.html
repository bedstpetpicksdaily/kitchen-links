Frequently Asked Questions

Will the nonstick coating actually last, or will it peel like my old cookware?

Hard anodized construction is fundamentally different from standard nonstick—the coating bonds more aggressively to the aluminum base, which is why it resists peeling and scratching significantly better. That said, avoid metal utensils and abrasive scrubbing, and use low-to-medium heat on your stovetop. Treated properly, this set should maintain its nonstick properties for 5+ years, which is roughly double what you get from cheaper nonstick alternatives.

Is the 12-piece set actually enough, or will I need to buy more pans separately?

For most home cooks, yes—12 pieces covers your essentials. You get two skillets (8-inch and 10-inch), three saucepans with lids, a Dutch oven, and supporting items. The real question is whether you cook complicated meals simultaneously or primarily handle one or two dishes at a time. If you're making pasta, sauce, and a protein in separate pans every night, you might want additional skillets, but that's an honest assessment of your kitchen habits, not a flaw in the set.

Does it work on all stovetops, or do I need to check compatibility first?

This set works on gas and electric stovetops without issue. Induction cooktops are the exception—hard anodized aluminum isn't magnetic, so the pans won't heat properly on induction surfaces. If your kitchen uses induction, check the product listing for induction-compatible options before committing. For traditional gas and electric ranges, you're completely compatible.

How does cleanup actually work, or will I still be scrubbing stuck food?

Nonstick handles most cleanup easily—food releases cleanly when the pan is properly preheated and you're using adequate fat. Hand wash recommended for maximum lifespan (dishwashers are technically okay but harder on the coating over time). For stuck-on residue, deglaze the hot pan with water or broth immediately after cooking rather than letting it cool and crust. Takes 60 seconds and beats scrubbing entirely.
