What to Look For

  • Capacity and Your Household Size – If you're cooking for 2-3 people, a 6-quart model offers flexibility without dominating your countertop. Families of 5+ should consider an 8-quart, while solo cooks can get away with a 3-quart. Think about whether you'll batch-cook or meal-prep; larger families benefit from bigger capacities that let you cook once and eat multiple times.
  • Cooking Functions That Match Your Style – All Instant Pots pressure cook and slow cook, but features differ. If you want to make yogurt, consider models with yogurt settings. If crispy food appeals to you, look for air fryer-compatible lids or combo units. If you're new to pressure cooking, simpler models with fewer buttons are less intimidating than advanced ones with 15+ functions.
  • Durability and Warranty Protection – The sealing ring and inner pot take the most wear over time. Choose models with stainless steel inner pots rather than non-stick (they last longer and handle acidic foods better). Check for at least a 2-year warranty; many quality brands offer up to 10 years on the cooker body, which signals confidence in longevity.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What size Instant Pot should I buy for a family of 4?

    A 6-quart Instant Pot is ideal for a family of 4, as it allows you to cook larger batches and store leftovers without needing multiple cooking cycles. If you primarily cook for 2-3 people or have limited counter/storage space, a 5.7-quart model offers a good balance between capacity and footprint.

    What's the difference between Instant Pot models and which features do I actually need?

    The main differences are cooking programs and smart features—basic models have 7 programs while premium models offer 15+ including sous vide and sterilize functions. For most home cooks, a mid-range model with pressure cook, slow cook, sauté, and steam functions is sufficient; advanced features like Wi-Fi connectivity are nice-to-have but rarely essential.

    Is Instant Pot worth it or are cheaper multi cookers just as good?

    Instant Pot has the most reliable safety features, consistent heating, and extensive recipe community support, making it worth the investment if you plan to use it regularly. Cheaper brands may work adequately, but Instant Pot's durability and customer service typically provide better long-term value.

    Can I use my Instant Pot for both pressure cooking and slow cooking?

    Yes, all Instant Pot models include both pressure cook and slow cook functions, so you get versatility in one appliance. This dual functionality makes it especially valuable for busy cooks who want to meal prep with different cooking methods throughout the week.
