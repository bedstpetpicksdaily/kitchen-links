How to Clean a Coffee Maker with Vinegar

To clean a coffee maker with vinegar, fill the water reservoir with a 1:1 mixture of white vinegar and water, run it through a brew cycle, let it sit for 15 minutes, then run plain water through 2-3 cycles to rinse. This simple process removes mineral buildup, bacteria, and improves your coffee's taste.

The Short Answer

Cleaning your coffee maker with vinegar is one of the easiest and most effective maintenance tasks you can do. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water, run it through a complete brew cycle, allow it to sit for 15 minutes, and then flush the system with plain water for 2-3 cycles. This removes limescale, mineral deposits, and odors that accumulate over time, keeping your coffee maker functioning properly and your coffee tasting fresh.

The Full Explanation

Why Vinegar Works

White vinegar is acidic with a pH around 2.4, making it ideal for dissolving mineral deposits like calcium and magnesium that build up inside your coffee maker. These minerals, called limescale, restrict water flow, reduce heating efficiency, and can create stale-tasting coffee. Vinegar breaks down these deposits without damaging internal components, making it safer than many commercial cleaners.

Step-by-Step Cleaning Process

  1. Prepare the Solution: Mix one part white vinegar with one part water in a separate container, then pour it into your coffee maker's water reservoir. A standard 12-cup maker uses about 60 ounces total, so mix 30 ounces vinegar with 30 ounces water.
  2. Run the Brew Cycle: Place an empty carafe on the warming plate and run a complete brew cycle. This allows the vinegar solution to flow through all internal components, dissolving mineral buildup throughout the machine.
  3. Let It Soak: After the cycle completes, turn off the coffee maker and let the vinegar solution sit in the carafe and internal lines for 15 minutes. This dwell time allows the acid to work on stubborn deposits.
  4. Rinse Thoroughly: Empty the carafe and refill the reservoir with fresh water only. Run 2-3 full brew cycles with plain water to flush out all vinegar residue. This step is crucial—any remaining vinegar will affect your next pot of coffee.
  5. Clean the Carafe and Filter: While the machine cycles, hand-wash your glass carafe with warm soapy water and rinse thoroughly. Replace any disposable filters or rinse reusable ones.

How Often to Clean

For regular maintenance, clean your coffee maker with vinegar every 30 days if you use it daily. However, if you live in an area with hard water (high mineral content), increase frequency to every 2-3 weeks. You'll notice your coffee maker needs cleaning if brew time slows down, water doesn't heat properly, or coffee tastes off.

Alternative Solutions

If you don't have white vinegar, you can use lemon juice or commercial coffee maker cleaning solutions. However, vinegar is the most cost-effective and readily available option. Avoid using apple cider vinegar or other colored vinegars, as they may leave stains or odors.
