How to Clean a Coffee Maker Properly

Clean your coffee maker by running a brew cycle with equal parts white vinegar and water, letting it sit for 15 minutes, then running plain water cycles until the vinegar smell is gone. For best results, repeat this descaling process monthly to remove mineral buildup and maintain optimal brewing performance.

The Short Answer

The most effective way to clean a coffee maker is through descaling with white vinegar and water. Fill the reservoir with equal parts vinegar and water, run a full brew cycle, let it sit for 15 minutes, then run 2-3 cycles of plain water to rinse. This removes mineral deposits, bacteria, and old coffee oils that accumulate over time. For drip machines, also wash the carafe and filter basket weekly with hot soapy water.

The Full Explanation

Daily Cleaning Routine

Start with basic maintenance after each use. Empty the used grounds and filter, then rinse the filter basket under warm water. Wash the glass carafe and lid with hot soapy water, or place them in the dishwasher if your model allows it. Wipe the exterior and heating plate with a damp cloth to prevent coffee stain buildup. This prevents bacteria growth and keeps your machine looking clean.

Weekly Deep Clean

Once a week, soak removable parts in hot water with a few drops of dish soap for 10-15 minutes. Use a soft brush or bottle cleaner to scrub the interior spray head and water tube where water doesn't normally reach. Pay special attention to the spray head holes, as they can become clogged with mineral deposits. Rinse everything thoroughly under running water until all soap residue is gone.

Monthly Descaling Process

This is the most critical step for coffee maker longevity. Fill your water reservoir with a 1:1 mixture of white vinegar and water. Place an empty carafe on the warming plate and run a full brew cycle, allowing the vinegar solution to flow through the machine. Once the cycle completes, turn off the machine and let the remaining solution sit in the carafe for 15 minutes—this extra time dissolves stubborn mineral deposits inside the heating element. After 15 minutes, turn the machine back on and complete the brew cycle.

Next, empty the carafe and run 2-3 full cycles using only fresh filtered water. This is essential—vinegar smell and residue must be completely removed. Run cycles until you no longer smell vinegar and the water runs clear. Some manufacturers recommend repeating the entire descaling process if your machine hasn't been cleaned in several months or if you have hard water.

For Specific Coffee Maker Types

Automatic drip machines follow the process above. For single-serve machines like Keurigs, run the vinegar cycle as described, then run multiple water cycles through the machine and also flush water through the needle that punctures the pods. French press owners should soak the carafe in hot vinegar water, scrub the mesh filter thoroughly, and rinse completely. Espresso machine users need to backflush the group head with water only (no vinegar) and soak the portafilter and basket in hot water with espresso machine cleaner.

What the Experts Say

Coffee equipment experts and appliance manufacturers universally recommend descaling every 1-3 months depending on water hardness in your area. The Specialty Coffee Association suggests that mineral buildup reduces heating efficiency, slows brew time, and negatively impacts flavor extraction.
