What to Look For

1. Oven Size and Pizza Capacity

Consider how many pizzas you'll realistically cook at once. Most residential models range from 12-16 inches for personal use to 18+ inches for entertaining groups. Measure your patio or backyard space and think about your typical guest count—an 18-inch oven is ideal for families and regular entertaining, while 12-14 inches works for couples or smaller spaces.

2. Build Material and Durability

The best wood-fired pizza ovens feature thick-walled construction with either stainless steel or ceramic exteriors paired with refractory brick interiors. Look for models with reinforced stainless steel frames and weather-resistant materials that can withstand year-round temperature fluctuations. Avoid thin-walled models that crack or rust quickly.

3. Heat Retention and Temperature Control

Superior heat retention means your oven reaches and maintains cooking temperatures with less wood, saving money long-term. Check specifications for maximum temperature capability (most quality models reach 800°F+) and look for ovens with proper insulation and thick floors that distribute heat evenly for consistent cooking.

4. Installation Type and Portability

Decide between free-standing models (easily relocated, no permanent installation) and built-in options (permanent installations that integrate with outdoor kitchens). Free-standing ovens offer flexibility if you might move; built-ins create a more polished, integrated aesthetic. Factor in whether you need a dedicated base, stand, or custom installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of wood should I use in a wood fired pizza oven?

Use seasoned hardwoods like oak, maple, hickory, or fruitwoods that have been dried for at least 6-12 months to a moisture content below 20%. Avoid softwoods like pine or treated lumber as they produce excessive smoke and creosote buildup. Hardwoods burn hotter and longer, giving you better pizza results with less frequent reloading.

How hot does a wood fired pizza oven get and how long does it take to heat up?

Quality wood fired pizza ovens reach 700-900°F (370-480°C), with some professional models exceeding 1000°F. Most home ovens take 30-45 minutes to preheat when you build a proper fire, though you can start cooking at 500°F if needed for longer cooking times.

How much space do I need for a wood fired pizza oven?

Plan for a minimum of 6 feet of clearance around the oven base and 10-12 feet overhead clearance to account for chimney height and heat rise. Most residential pizza ovens have a footprint of 3-5 feet in diameter or width, so a dedicated garden space of at least 10x10 feet is ideal.

What's the difference between a wood fired pizza oven and a regular grill?

Wood fired pizza ovens retain intense radiant heat in their domed chambers, cooking pizzas in 60-90 seconds at very high temperatures, while grills distribute heat more gradually and unevenly. Pizza ovens have a chimney system that circulates heat efficiently and can reach much higher temperatures, making them purpose-built for pizzas rather than multi-use cooking.
