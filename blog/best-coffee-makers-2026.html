Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between espresso and drip coffee makers?

Espresso machines use high pressure to force hot water through tightly packed grounds, creating a concentrated shot with a rich crema layer. Drip coffee makers use gravity to slowly filter water through grounds, producing a milder, larger volume of coffee. Espresso machines are better for short, intense drinks, while drip makers are ideal for filling multiple cups.

How often should I clean my coffee maker?

For daily-use coffee makers, you should rinse the filter basket and carafe daily. Weekly deep cleaning with a damp cloth is recommended, and a monthly descaling cycle (using white vinegar or a commercial descaling solution) removes mineral buildup that affects taste and machine performance. Single-serve pod machines should be cleaned weekly according to manufacturer instructions.
