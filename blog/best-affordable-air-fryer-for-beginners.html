What to Look For

  • Capacity Between 3-5 Quarts: Beginners don't need massive cooking space yet. A 3.5 to 5-quart basket is perfect for individuals, couples, or small families while keeping the price down and taking up minimal counter space.
  • Simple, Intuitive Controls: Skip the digital touchscreen models with 15 preset functions. Look for straightforward dials or basic buttons for temperature and time—you want to focus on learning to cook, not deciphering a complicated interface.
  • Honest Wattage (1400-1700W): Lower wattage doesn't mean worse results, but you want enough power to crisp food properly. Models in this range deliver consistent heating without being overly complicated or expensive.
