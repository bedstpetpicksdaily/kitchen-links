Meal prep can be a game-changer for busy weekdays, but only if you have the right tools in your kitchen. What to Look For

  • Capacity that matches your household size: For meal prep, you'll want at least a 6-quart Instant Pot if you're cooking for 2-4 people regularly. This size allows you to cook enough chicken, rice, or beans in one batch to last several days without needing multiple cooking cycles. An 8-quart model works best if you're prepping for a larger family or want to freeze portions.
  • Multiple cooking functions beyond pressure cooking: Look for models with sauté, slow cook, and steaming capabilities built-in. These features let you brown meat before pressure cooking, cook delicate grains perfectly, or steam vegetables without needing separate cookware—all essential for efficient meal prep workflows.
  • Easy-to-read display and simple preset buttons: During meal prep marathons, you'll appreciate clear markings, intuitive controls, and helpful preset buttons for common foods like rice, beans, and poultry. This reduces guesswork and lets you focus on prep work rather than fiddling with settings.
