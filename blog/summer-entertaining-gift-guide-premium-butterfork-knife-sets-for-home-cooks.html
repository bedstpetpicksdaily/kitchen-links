Frequently Asked Questions

What's included in a typical Butterfork knife set?

Butterfork knife sets are thoughtfully curated and may include chef knives, bread knives, paring knives, and sometimes specialized options like nakiri knives. The specific contents vary by collection, with sets designed to provide essential pieces without unnecessary duplication. Many sets are complemented by knife stands for convenient storage and display.

How do I care for my Butterfork knives?

Butterfork knives should be hand-washed with warm soapy water and dried immediately—avoid dishwashers as they damage the blades. Regular honing with a honing steel keeps the edge sharp, while occasional professional sharpening maintains peak performance. Storing them in a knife block or stand protects both the blades and your hands.

Are Butterfork knives suitable for professional cooks?

While Butterfork primarily targets serious home cooks, their professional-grade construction, blade quality, and edge retention make them suitable for anyone who takes cooking seriously. Many experienced home cooks and culinary enthusiasts prefer Butterfork knives for their balance of quality and value.

What's the difference between Butterfork's chef knife and nakiri knife?

Chef knives are curved-blade workhorse knives excellent for rocking cuts, chopping, and general tasks. Nakiri knives feature a straighter blade and flatter profile, making them ideal for slicing vegetables with a push-cut motion. Both serve different purposes and many cooks appreciate having both in their collection.

Do Butterfork knife sets come with a warranty?

Warranty details vary by product and collection. We recommend checking the specific product page or contacting Butterfork directly for comprehensive warranty information, as coverage may differ for various knife types and sets.
