What to Look For

  • Basket capacity between 3-5 quarts: This size range offers the sweet spot for budget models—large enough to cook meals for 2-4 people without the premium price tag of massive 6+ quart units. You'll still get crispy results with proper air circulation while keeping costs down.
  • Simple, reliable controls: Skip models with fancy touchscreens and preset programs that inflate the price. A basic temperature dial, timer knob, and power button are all you need for crispy cooking. Simpler mechanics mean fewer parts that can break and lower repair costs.
  • Wide temperature range (300-400°F minimum): You need flexibility to cook everything from delicate vegetables to thick-cut chicken thighs. Budget air fryers that hit at least 400°F will give you the high heat necessary for that golden, crispy exterior while keeping interiors juicy.

  • Ultra-cheap models under $40: While the price is tempting, air fryers in this range often have uneven heating, weak air circulation, and durability issues. You'll end up with soggy spots, burnt edges, and frustrating results that make you regret the purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest air fryer that actually works well?

Budget air fryers in the $50-80 range from brands like Cosori, Instant Pot, and Dreo offer solid performance for home cooking without breaking the bank. Look for models with at least 3.5-4 quart capacity and 1400+ watts of power, which will give you crispy results comparable to more expensive options. Avoid ultra-cheap models under $40 as they often have weak heating elements and inconsistent cooking.

Can you get crispy food in a budget air fryer or do you need an expensive one?

Budget air fryers produce equally crispy food as expensive models because crispiness depends on circulating hot air, not price—all air fryers work on the same principle. The difference with pricier models is usually extra features like smart controls or larger capacity, not cooking quality. A $60 air fryer will crisp chicken wings and fries just as well as a $200 one.

What size air fryer should I buy if I'm on a budget?

For a single person or couple, a 3-quart model is the most affordable sweet spot at $50-70 and handles most meals efficiently. If you have a family of 4+, invest the extra $20-30 for a 5-6 quart model to avoid cooking in batches, which saves time and energy costs. Anything smaller than 3 quarts will require multiple batches for most meals, defeating the budget-friendly purpose.

Are there air fryers under $50 that won't break after a few months?

Yes, but you need to be selective—brands like Cosori, Instant Pot, and Chefman have reliable $50-60 models with good warranty coverage and customer reviews. Avoid no-name brands from Amazon that seem suspiciously cheap, as they have higher failure rates and poor customer support. Spending $60-80 on an established brand gives you better durability than trying to save $10-20 on an unknown model.
