No, you do not need a separate pot for air fryer cooking—most air fryers come with their own cooking basket or tray. However, investing in air fryer-specific cookware can expand your cooking options and improve results for certain dishes.

The Short Answer

Air fryers arrive equipped with a basket or tray designed for their specific size and airflow pattern. For basic air frying, this is all you need. However, dedicated air fryer cookware—like perforated pans, cake tins, and silicone molds—can enhance versatility and allow you to cook items that wouldn't work in the standard basket alone. These accessories aren't essential but are highly convenient for certain recipes.

The Full Explanation

Every air fryer includes a cooking basket or tray as its primary cooking vessel. This basket is engineered to allow hot air circulation around your food, which is the core mechanism that makes air frying work. The perforations or mesh design of the basket is specifically calibrated for your air fryer model, so it performs optimally for standard air frying tasks like cooking frozen fries, chicken wings, or vegetables.

The question of whether you need additional cookware depends on your cooking ambitions. If you plan to air fry only standard foods—proteins, vegetables, and frozen items—the included basket is sufficient. However, air fryer accessory sets open up new possibilities. You might want to bake a small cake, prepare lasagna, cook pizza, or steam dumplings in your air fryer. For these tasks, the standard basket may not be ideal or functional.

Additional air fryer cookware typically includes items such as:

  • Round cake tins: For baking cakes, brownies, and bread in your air fryer
  • Perforated metal pans: For reheating foods or cooking items that need drainage
  • Silicone molds: For making egg muffins, individual portions, or delicate items
  • Pizza pans: Specifically sized to fit your air fryer for homemade pizza
  • Grill inserts: For cooking foods that need grill marks without falling through the basket

The key consideration is that any cookware you use in an air fryer must be heat-safe and allow adequate airflow. Unlike traditional ovens, air fryers rely on rapid hot air circulation, so your cookware choice matters more. Solid-bottomed pans can work, but they may extend cooking times slightly since the air cannot circulate underneath the food as efficiently.

Consumer Reports and cooking websites suggest that dedicated air fryer cookware sets represent a worthwhile investment for people who use their air fryer multiple times weekly.
