What to Look For

  • Wattage between 1000-1200W – This range ensures consistent heating and reliable pressure buildup, which is crucial for beginner recipes.   • Preset programs for common one-pot meals – Look for built-in buttons for rice, soup, stew, and meat. Beginners benefit enormously from these foolproof presets that take guesswork out of cooking times and pressure levels, so you're not second-guessing yourself on a Tuesday night.
  • A 6-quart capacity minimum – This size gives you enough room to layer ingredients properly (which prevents burning) and feeds a family without requiring a second batch. Smaller 3-quart models can feel limiting once you realize how versatile these cookers are.

What to Avoid

  • Don't choose ultra-compact 3-quart models for family dinners – While they seem convenient, they're genuinely frustrating for cooking enough food for four or more people. You'll find yourself making two batches, which defeats the whole "one-pot, one dinner" appeal and doubles your cleanup.
