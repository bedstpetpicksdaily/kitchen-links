What to Look For

  • Stainless Steel Construction with Non-Slip Feet — Choose trivets made from food-grade stainless steel that won't rust or leach into your food. Non-slip silicone feet are crucial; they prevent the trivet from sliding around during cooking and protect your inner pot from scratches that can compromise its integrity over time.
  • Multiple Heights and Configurations — The best trivet sets include stackable pieces or adjustable heights so you can create different cooking zones. This flexibility lets you simultaneously steam vegetables on top while pressure cooking a sauce below, or cook two separate dishes in different-sized containers at once.
  • Compatibility with Your Pot Size — Verify that the trivets fit your specific Instant Pot model (6-quart vs. 8-quart) with adequate clearance. A trivet that's too large will jam against the sides and prevent the lid from sealing; too small and your cookware won't have stable support, risking spills and uneven cooking.

What to Avoid

  • Flimsy Aluminum or Silicone-Only Trivets — While budget-friendly, aluminum trivets can bend under the weight of full containers and won't withstand years of pressure cooking. Pure silicone trivets lack structural integrity for heavy cookware and can absorb odors and stains that permanently affect appearance and performance.
  • Generic Round Racks Without Handles — Standard cooling racks or generic kitchen trivets often lack the handles essential for safely removing hot cookware from a pressurized pot.     Frequently Asked Questions

    What size trivet do I need for my Instant Pot?

    Most standard Instant Pots (6-quart and 8-quart) use trivets that are approximately 8.5 inches in diameter and 1-1.5 inches tall. Before purchasing, check your Instant Pot model's manual or measure your pot's interior to ensure the trivet fits without touching the heating element or lid. Oversized trivets can prevent the lid from closing properly, so accurate sizing is essential.

    Do I really need a trivet or can I use something else?

    While you can use makeshift alternatives like aluminum foil balls or small jars, a proper trivet is specifically designed to elevate food safely and maintain even heat distribution. Dedicated trivets prevent food from burning on the bottom and are reusable, making them more economical and reliable than improvised options.

    Can I use metal and silicone trivets interchangeably?

    Both metal and silicone trivets work in Instant Pots, but they have different advantages: metal trivets conduct heat better for steaming and are more durable, while silicone trivets prevent scratching and are quieter when placing dishes. Choose metal for steaming vegetables and silicone for baking cheesecakes or delicate items you want to protect from direct heat contact.

    What accessories should I pair with my trivet for better cooking?

    Stacking racks, silicone egg bite molds, and springform pan inserts pair perfectly with trivets to cook multiple items simultaneously. A quality multi-use trivet set that includes these accessories allows you to steam, bake, and roast all in one pot, maximizing your Instant Pot's versatility.

    Who Should Buy This

    If you're an Instant Pot enthusiast who regularly cooks multiple dishes, makes cheesecakes, or uses your cooker several times a week, a quality trivet set is a worthwhile investment. These accessories are especially valuable for anyone who wants to master stacking and layering techniques, meal prep in batches, or cook food that requires elevation off the bottom for even cooking.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • Buying trivets that don't fit your specific Instant Pot size—always verify compatibility before purchasing, as 6-quart models need different trivets than 8-quart versions.
    • Assuming all trivets are created equal; cheap trivets can warp under pressure or have sharp edges that damage your pot's interior, so invest in stainless steel options with smooth finishes.
    • Neglecting to clean and dry trivets immediately after use—moisture left between the trivet and pot bottom can cause rust spots and reduce the lifespan of your accessories.

    Expert Tips for Best Results

    • Use the rack side down for standard cooking and stackable side up when layering multiple foods, allowing steam to circulate properly between layers for even cooking times.
    • Place parchment paper or foil on trivets when cooking delicate foods like fish or cheesecake to prevent sticking and make cleanup effortless—this simple step transforms your cooking versatility.
    • Keep your trivet set organized in a dedicated drawer or hanging organizer near your Instant Pot so you'll actually reach for them instead of cooking everything directly on the bottom.
