Best for: Budget-conscious consumers unconcerned with environmental impact; situations requiring truly single-use, disposable solutions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a single Bee's Wrap actually last?

With proper care, Bee's Wrap lasts approximately one year of regular use. Lifespan depends on washing frequency, water temperature (cool water only), and storage conditions. Improper washing or high heat degrades the beeswax coating significantly.

Can I use Bee's Wrap to cover hot foods or in the freezer?

No. Bee's Wrap cannot withstand hot temperatures above room temperature—beeswax melts. It also should not be used in freezers, as cold causes the beeswax coating to crack and lose effectiveness. Use only for room-temperature storage.

Are Bee's Wrap products food-safe and non-toxic?

Yes. Bee's Wrap is made from organic cotton infused with sustainably harvested beeswax, plant oil, and tree resin—no synthetic adhesives or chemicals. The formulation is food-safe and compostable at end-of-life.

What sizes do Bee's Wrap come in, and what foods work best?

Bee's Wrap comes in multiple sizes to accommodate different needs—individual sandwich wraps, medium bowl covers, and large wrap options. Works best for room-temperature foods: sandwiches, cheese, fresh produce, and covering bowls of salads or leftovers. Less effective for wet foods that might slide around.

How do I clean and care for Bee's Wrap to maximize lifespan?

Hand wash with cool water and plant-based soap only. Hot water and dishwashers degrade the beeswax coating rapidly. Air dry completely before storing. Avoid folding in the same spot repeatedly, as creases can cause cracking. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct heat or sunlight.

Is Bee's Wrap actually cheaper than plastic wrap over time?

Yes, if you achieve one year of lifespan. A single Bee's Wrap ($5–15) replaces hundreds of sheets of plastic wrap over 12 months, making per-use cost substantially lower. However, upfront cost is higher, requiring initial investment acceptance.

Can I gift Bee's Wrap to someone unfamiliar with sustainable products?

Yes, but include a brief care instruction card. The learning curve is minimal—it functions intuitively like plastic wrap but requires cool-water handwashing. Frame it as a practical upgrade rather than a lifestyle lecture for best reception.
