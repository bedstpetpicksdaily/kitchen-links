What to Look For

  • Versatility vs. Simplicity: Instant Pots function as 7-in-1 or 11-in-1 multi-cookers with preset programs for pressure cooking, slow cooking, rice cooking, steaming, sautéing, yogurt making, and more. Traditional stovetop pressure cookers do one thing exceptionally well—pressure cook—but require manual monitoring and adjustment. Consider whether you want a single-purpose workhorse or a kitchen all-rounder.
  • Learning Curve and Safety Features: Electric Instant Pots have automatic pressure release, multiple safety valves, and digital controls that make them nearly impossible to misuse. Stovetop pressure cookers require you to monitor heat, understand venting, and manually release pressure, which means they demand more skill and attention. If you're new to pressure cooking or prefer a set-and-forget approach, the Instant Pot's automation is invaluable.
  • Cooking Speed and Heat Control: Instant Pots reach pressure more slowly but maintain consistent heat and are ideal for braises, stews, and batch cooking. Stovetop models reach full pressure faster and give you more direct control over heat intensity, making them better for experienced cooks who want precision. Think about whether you prioritize convenience or hands-on control.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What's the difference between an Instant Pot and a regular pressure cooker?

    An Instant Pot is a brand of electric pressure cooker with built-in safety features and multiple preset cooking programs, while traditional pressure cookers are stovetop-based and require manual monitoring. Instant Pots also function as slow cookers, rice cookers, and steamers, making them more versatile than basic pressure cookers. Traditional pressure cookers typically heat faster and are less expensive, but require more hands-on attention during cooking.

    Is an Instant Pot worth the money compared to other multi cookers?

    Instant Pot tends to be mid-range in price ($80-150) with excellent reliability and a massive recipe community online, making it easier to troubleshoot and find meal inspiration. Other brands like Ninja and Cosori offer similar features at comparable or slightly lower prices with different preset programs. The choice depends on your budget and whether you prefer Instant Pot's established reputation or want to explore other brands' unique features.

    Can I use a pressure cooker for all the same recipes as an Instant Pot?

    Most pressure cooker recipes can be used in both, but Instant Pot recipes written for specific presets and features (like saute mode) may need adjustments for stovetop cookers. Instant Pot's slower heating and multiple cooking programs make it more forgiving for beginners, while stovetop pressure cookers require you to manually manage temperature and timing. If you find most recipes you want to try are Instant Pot-specific, that's worth considering in your decision.

    Which multi cooker is easiest to use for beginners?

    The Instant Pot is generally easier for beginners because it has preset buttons, automatic pressure release options, and built-in safety features that prevent user error. Stovetop pressure cookers have a steeper learning curve since you need to monitor heat levels, manually release pressure, and judge cooking times without electronic guidance. If ease of use is your priority, an electric multi cooker like Instant Pot will be more forgiving as you're learning.
