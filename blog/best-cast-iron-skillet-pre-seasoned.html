Frequently Asked Questions

Do pre-seasoned cast iron skillets need to be seasoned before first use?

Pre-seasoned cast iron has a factory-applied protective layer, so you can use it immediately for cooking. However, many cooks recommend seasoning it one more time at home to build up the non-stick surface faster and ensure even coverage.

How long does pre-seasoned cast iron seasoning last?

Factory seasoning is typically thinner than well-maintained home seasoning and can wear off within months of regular use. The good news is that every time you cook with oil or fat, you're adding to the seasoning, so it improves over time with proper care.

Is pre-seasoned cast iron better than unseasoned for beginners?

Yes, pre-seasoned cast iron is ideal for beginners because it's ready to use immediately and has a head start on non-stick performance. You avoid the intimidating initial seasoning process while still being able to maintain and improve the seasoning yourself.

Can you use soap on pre-seasoned cast iron?

Modern dish soaps are gentler than old-fashioned lye-based soaps, so using a small amount of mild soap on pre-seasoned cast iron is generally safe. The key is to dry it thoroughly immediately after washing and apply a thin coat of oil to maintain the seasoning.
