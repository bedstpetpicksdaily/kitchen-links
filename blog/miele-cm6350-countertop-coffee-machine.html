Frequently Asked Questions

How does the CM6350 compare to manual espresso machines?

The CM6350 eliminates tamping, pressure guessing, and milk steaming technique. A manual machine gives you more control and ritual, but demands real skill. Choose the CM6350 if consistency and speed matter more than the hands-on learning process. Manual machines are better if you enjoy the craft element.

What's the actual maintenance like month-to-month?

Weekly cleaning involves running the cleaning cycle (automatic) and emptying the grounds container. Monthly, you'll run a descale program with cleaning tablets. It's far less hands-on than semi-automatic machines, but more involved than a pod system. Budget 10-15 minutes weekly.

Can this machine handle different milk types for lattes and cappuccinos?

Yes. The automatic milk frother works with regular dairy, oat, almond, and soy milk. Oat milk actually froths beautifully on this model, which isn't true for every machine. You can manually adjust frothing intensity if the default doesn't match your preference.

Is the grinder quality good enough to justify the built-in design?

The integrated grinder is solid but not professional-grade. It's consistent enough for home use and adapts well to different beans. However, serious coffee nerds sometimes swap in external grinders later. For most users, the built-in grinder is reliable and convenient enough to not feel limiting.
