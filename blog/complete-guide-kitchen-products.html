Frequently Asked Questions

What kitchen knives do I actually need as a home cook?

You really only need three essential knives: an 8-inch chef's knife for chopping vegetables and meat, a paring knife for smaller tasks, and a serrated bread knife. A quality chef's knife will handle 90% of your cutting work, so invest in one good knife rather than a full block of mediocre ones.

Is a stand mixer worth it or can I just use a hand mixer?

A stand mixer is worth it if you bake regularly or make dough, as it saves time and effort for heavy mixing tasks. If you only bake occasionally or prefer quick recipes, a hand mixer or even mixing by hand will work fine and saves counter space.

What size cutting board should I buy?

Get at least one cutting board that's 12x18 inches or larger, as this gives you enough workspace for prep without ingredients rolling off. For a home cook, having two boards (one for vegetables and one for meat) is ideal for food safety without needing excessive storage space.

Do I need both a colander and a strainer?

No, you can start with just a colander (large holes) for draining pasta and vegetables, which is the more versatile tool. A fine-mesh strainer is helpful for sifting flour or draining canned goods, but it's not essential until you cook more frequently.
