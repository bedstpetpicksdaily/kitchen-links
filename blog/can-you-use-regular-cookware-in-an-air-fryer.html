Yes, you can use regular cookware in an air fryer, but with important limitations. Not all materials are safe or compatible with air fryer cooking, and success depends on the specific cookware type and your air fryer model.

The Short Answer

Regular cookware can work in an air fryer if it's oven-safe and fits properly, but glass, ceramic, and metal containers require careful consideration. Your best bet is to check if the cookware is labeled oven-safe at temperatures above 350°F, ensure it fits your air fryer basket without blocking air circulation, and test it with a small batch first. Some materials like non-stick plastic handles or certain coatings may warp or release harmful chemicals at air fryer temperatures.

The Full Explanation

Air fryers work by circulating extremely hot air at high speeds around food, typically reaching 350-400°F. This means any cookware you use must withstand these temperatures without degrading, melting, or releasing toxins.

Metal Cookware: Stainless steel and aluminum pans generally work well in air fryers since they're designed to handle high heat. However, aluminum can react with acidic foods, and some aluminum pieces may have handles that aren't heat-resistant. Always verify that handles are securely attached and made from heat-safe materials.

Glass and Ceramic: Oven-safe glass and ceramic dishes can work, but sudden temperature changes can cause thermal shock and cracking. Never move a cold glass dish into a preheated air fryer. Ceramic glazes may contain lead, so ensure any ceramic is specifically labeled as food-safe and oven-safe.

Non-stick Cookware: This is the trickiest category. Standard non-stick coatings can start degrading at temperatures above 500°F, but since most air fryers max out around 400°F, many non-stick pans are technically safe. The real concern is whether the pan fits your air fryer without blocking airflow—most non-stick cookware is too large. Additionally, heating non-stick cookware to its upper temperature limits repeatedly can shorten its lifespan.

Silicone and Plastics: Regular silicone is typically oven-safe up to 400°F, making it compatible with most air fryers. However, avoid plastic cookware entirely unless it's specifically rated for air fryer use—most will melt or warp. Plastic utensils and handles on cookware items are particularly problematic.

The Size Problem: Even if your cookware is heat-safe, it might be too large for your air fryer basket. Air circulation is critical to air frying's success; cramped cookware can cause uneven cooking and even damage your machine. Your cookware should be at least 1 inch smaller on all sides than your air fryer basket.

What the Experts Say

Kitchen appliance experts consistently recommend prioritizing air fryer-specific cookware over adapting regular kitchen items. The consensus is that while some regular cookware works acceptably, purpose-built air fryer accessories are designed with air circulation in mind and eliminate guesswork. Many professional reviewers note that using oversized or incompatible cookware is the leading cause of poor air fryer results, including unevenly cooked food and potential damage to the heating element.
