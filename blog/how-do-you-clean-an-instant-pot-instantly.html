How to Clean an Instant Pot Instantly

The fastest way to clean an Instant Pot instantly is to rinse the inner pot with hot water immediately after cooking, soak stubborn food debris for 15 minutes, and wipe down the exterior and accessories with a damp cloth. Most daily cleaning can be completed in under 5 minutes using this quick method.

The Short Answer

An Instant Pot isn't difficult to clean if you act quickly after cooking. Start by removing the inner pot and rinsing it under hot running water while food is still warm and hasn't hardened. For stuck-on food, add warm water and a drop of dish soap directly to the pot, let it sit for 15 minutes, then scrub with a soft sponge. The removable accessories—like the sealing ring and anti-block shield—should be rinsed separately, and the exterior can be wiped clean with a damp microfiber cloth in seconds.

The Full Explanation

Immediate Cleaning Process: The key to instant cleaning is speed. As soon as you finish cooking and release the pressure, remove the inner pot from the heating element. This prevents food from continuing to cook and hardening onto the bottom. Rinse the pot under hot water immediately, using a soft sponge or cloth to wipe away any residue while it's still warm.

Dealing with Stuck-On Food: If you encounter stubborn, stuck-on food, don't let it sit for hours. Instead, fill the inner pot halfway with warm water and add a few drops of dish soap. Let it soak for 10-15 minutes while the food softens. The warm water rehydrates dried food, making it much easier to remove. After soaking, use a non-abrasive sponge or silicone scraper to gently lift away the debris. Avoid steel wool or harsh scouring pads, as these can scratch the pot's non-stick coating.

Sealing Ring and Accessories: The sealing ring requires special attention because it absorbs food odors and flavors. Remove it after every use and rinse it under warm water with a small amount of dish soap. If it develops stubborn stains or odors, soak it separately in warm soapy water. The anti-block shield and pressure release valve should also be rinsed with warm water and checked for any food particles.

Exterior and Lid Cleaning: The exterior stainless steel housing and the glass or steel lid can be wiped clean in seconds with a damp microfiber cloth. For fingerprints on stainless steel, use a microfiber cloth dampened with a tiny amount of mineral oil or stainless steel cleaner. The lid's interior should be wiped with a damp cloth to remove any moisture or food splatters.

Dishwasher-Safe Parts: The removable inner pot, sealing ring, anti-block shield, and pressure release valve are all top-rack dishwasher safe on most Instant Pot models. If you prefer the convenience of a dishwasher, you can load these parts immediately after rinsing away excess food. However, hand-washing with warm soapy water is actually faster for daily cleaning.

What the Experts Say

Instant Pot manufacturers and kitchen experts consistently recommend cleaning the appliance immediately after cooking for the fastest results. Professional kitchen reviewers note that the 15-minute "instant" clean method is actually faster than loading a dishwasher, especially since you still need to rinse the pot before placing it in the machine. Cleaning experts emphasize that warm water—not cold—is the secret to quick cleaning, as it keeps food particles pliable and easier to remove.
