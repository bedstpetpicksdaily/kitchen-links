What to Look For

  • Size and Capacity: Instant Pots range from 3-quarts to 10-quarts. A 6-quart model is ideal for most households of 4-6 people and offers enough space for bulk cooking without taking over your counter. If you live alone or cook for two, a 3-quart saves space. Families larger than six or serious meal preppers should consider the 8 or 10-quart models. Think about your counter space honestly—an appliance you can't store or display comfortably won't get used.
  • Control Type and Smart Features: Instant Pots come in mechanical dial versions and smart LED touch-panel models that connect to apps. Smart models offer convenience and recipe guidance but cost more and require troubleshooting if the electronics fail. Mechanical models are simpler, more durable long-term, and perfectly adequate for most cooking. Choose based on whether you want preset programs or prefer hands-on control—there's no wrong answer, only what fits your cooking style.
  • Stainless Steel Quality and Interior Finish: Look for 18/8 stainless steel (containing 18% chromium and 8% nickel) for durability and resistance to corrosion. A brushed or polished exterior looks professional in any kitchen, while the inner pot should be 3-ply stainless steel for even heat distribution. Avoid models with cheaper aluminum cores that dent easily or non-stick interiors that degrade after repeated use and high-heat cooking.
