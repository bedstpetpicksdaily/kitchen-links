Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use different temperatures in each basket simultaneously?

Yes, and this is what separates it from cheaper dual-basket models. Each basket has an independent digital control panel with its own temperature setting and timer. You can run the top basket at 350°F for delicate fish while the bottom basket crisps root vegetables at 420°F. The heating elements are electrically independent, so there's no compromise in either basket's performance.

How much counter space does it actually take up?

The footprint is approximately 16.5" x 15", roughly the same as a standard microwave. Height is the trade-off—it sits about 18" tall with the stacking design. If you have vertical clearance above your counter, this actually saves space compared to two traditional side-by-side air fryers, which would occupy roughly 32" of linear counter depth.

Is it worth the premium over single-basket Ninja models?

That depends entirely on your cooking volume. For a family of 4+ eating together regularly, or for anyone doing serious meal prep, the time savings justify the cost within a year. You're eliminating the need to cook in batches. For solo cooks or couples, a standard Ninja air fryer ($80-120) handles 95% of your needs. The Double Stack XL ($300+) is targeted at volume cooking, and it excels there. The 4.3-star rating shows users who match the intended use case are genuinely satisfied.

Can you really remove the divider and use it as one large fryer?

Yes, but with caveats. The FlexBasket design lets you remove the partition for 9.5 total quarts of cooking space. However, you then lose independent temperature control—the unit will cook at the temperature set by one control panel. For large items like whole chickens or cookie sheets of fries, this works beautifully. For mixed cooking (different temps), this defeats the purpose. Think of it as a bonus feature, not the primary selling point.
