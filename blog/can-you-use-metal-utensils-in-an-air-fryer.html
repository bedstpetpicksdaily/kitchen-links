Can You Use Metal Utensils in an Air Fryer?

Yes, you can use metal utensils in an air fryer, but it depends on the type of metal and your air fryer's interior coating. Stainless steel utensils are generally safe, while materials like aluminum or utensils with non-stick coatings require more caution.

The Short Answer

Metal utensils are safe to use in air fryers as long as they won't damage the interior coating or interfere with the heating elements. Stainless steel tongs and spatulas are your best bet for safe, durable cooking. Avoid using sharp metal utensils that could scratch non-stick surfaces, and never use utensils that are wider than your air fryer basket opening. Always check your air fryer's manual for manufacturer recommendations before using any metal tools.

"Metal utensils are perfectly safe to use in air fryers since the heating element doesn't operate on the same electromagnetic principle as microwave ovens, though I recommend using silicone-coated or stainless steel utensils to avoid scratching the non-stick basket coating, which can compromise its durability over time."

The Full Explanation

Why Metal Utensils Can Work in Air Fryers

Unlike microwave ovens, air fryers don't have electromagnetic fields that react dangerously with metal. Air fryers work by circulating extremely hot air around food to create a crispy exterior through rapid heat circulation. This means metal itself isn't the problem—the real concerns are whether the utensil will damage your air fryer's interior or affect cooking performance.

The Interior Coating Factor

Most modern air fryers feature non-stick coatings similar to those on non-stick cookware. These coatings, typically made from materials like ceramic or PTFE-based compounds, can be scratched or damaged by sharp metal utensils. Once the coating is compromised, it may flake into your food or reduce the air fryer's non-stick properties. This is the primary reason manufacturers often recommend against metal utensils—not because of safety hazards, but to preserve the appliance's longevity and performance.

Types of Metal Utensils and Safety

Stainless steel utensils are the safest metal option for air fryers. They're durable, won't react with high heat, and are less likely to scratch coatings if used carefully. Aluminum utensils are generally acceptable but may be softer and could potentially warp under extreme heat. Avoid thin, sharp-edged metal utensils that could easily scratch the basket or heating element area. Silicone-coated metal utensils offer a middle ground, protecting both the utensil and your air fryer's interior.

Size and Access Considerations

Even if metal is safe for your specific air fryer model, ensure the utensil is appropriately sized for your basket. Oversized utensils can obstruct airflow, which is essential for even cooking and the air fryer's proper function. They may also contact heating elements or damage the basket's edge. Always leave adequate space for air circulation when inserting any utensil into the cooking chamber.

What the Experts Say

Leading kitchen appliance manufacturers and reviewers generally agree that metal utensils can be used in air fryers with proper precautions. Consumer Reports notes that stainless steel tools are acceptable for air fryer use, while cautioning against sharp instruments that might damage non-stick surfaces. Most air fryer instruction manuals recommend using wooden, silicone, or plastic utensils as the first choice, but acknowledge that occasional use of smooth metal utensils won't damage the appliance. The consensus is to prioritize protecting the non-stick coating, as this extends your air fryer's useful life and maintains optimal performance.
