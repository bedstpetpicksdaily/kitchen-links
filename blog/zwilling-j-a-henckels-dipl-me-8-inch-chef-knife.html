Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to Victorinox Fibrox at similar price points?

Victorinox is Swiss, known for durability and ease of maintenance, while Zwilling is German, traditionally known for edge sharpness and steel quality. Both are solid budget choices, but Zwilling edges out Victorinox in initial sharpness, while Victorinox holds its edge longer with less maintenance. Your choice depends on whether you prefer a sharp knife that needs more honing or a durable knife that stays 'good enough' longer.

Is German stainless steel actually better than regular stainless steel?

German stainless steel (typically 14% chromium) offers better edge retention and corrosion resistance than lower-grade stainless alloys, but it's still not as hard as high-carbon or Japanese stainless steels. For a $10 knife, you're getting the good stuff—just not the premium stuff. It won't rust in your dishwasher, and it will hold an edge longer than cheap alternatives.

Does this knife need hand-washing, or can I put it in the dishwasher?

Hand-washing is always the safest option for any blade to preserve the edge and prevent corrosion from dishwasher detergent. That said, the stainless steel construction on this knife can technically survive a dishwasher better than many competitors. We recommend hand-washing during the first few months to establish good habits; after that, if you're careless, the knife will survive—but your edge will suffer.

What does 'full-tang' mean and why does it matter?

Full-tang means the metal blade extends all the way through the handle and is riveted in place. This creates a more durable, balanced knife that won't snap at the handle and distributes weight evenly. It's the difference between a knife that feels like a weapon and a knife that feels like a toy. Full-tang is the only design worth buying, and this Zwilling includes it.
