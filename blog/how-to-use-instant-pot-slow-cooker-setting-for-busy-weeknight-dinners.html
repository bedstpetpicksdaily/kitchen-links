What to Look For

  • Reliable slow cooker mode with consistent temperature control: Not all Instant Pots maintain steady, low heat equally well. Look for models specifically praised for even slow cooking performance—this prevents the "too hot" problem that can turn your dinner into mush or cause uneven cooking.
  • Adequate capacity for family-sized portions: A 6 or 8-quart model works best for weeknight dinners because you can cook enough for leftovers and have flexibility with ingredient quantities. Smaller 3-quart models require constant recipe scaling and won't feed most families comfortably.
  • Simple, intuitive controls and preset programs: After a long day, you need an appliance that doesn't require a manual. Choose models with clearly labeled slow cook buttons and straightforward temperature/time settings so you can set it and forget it without second-guessing yourself.

What to Avoid

  • Don't use the slow cooker setting for recipes requiring browning or searing: The Instant Pot's slow cook function doesn't develop the deep flavor that comes from searing meat first. If a recipe specifically calls for browning ingredients, use the sauté function before switching to slow cook, or choose recipes designed specifically for slow cooking from the start.
  • Avoid lifting the lid frequently to check on dinner: Unlike traditional slow cookers where peeking adds a minute or two, opening an Instant Pot during slow cooking can significantly extend your cooking time and affect temperature consistency. Trust the timer and resist the urge to check.
  • Don't try to cook frozen meat directly on the low setting: While the Instant Pot pressure function handles frozen meat beautifully, the slow cooker setting doesn't reach temperatures fast enough to safely thaw and cook frozen proteins. Thaw your meat overnight or use the pressure setting if you're in a pinch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use the Instant Pot slow cooker setting without a pressure cooker lid?

Yes, the Instant Pot slow cooker setting works with the standard tempered glass lid that comes with most models. You do not need the pressure cooking lid for slow cooking, making it convenient to switch between cooking methods without extra attachments.

How long does meat take to cook on Instant Pot slow cooker setting?

Cooking times vary based on meat type and size, but generally beef chuck takes 6-8 hours on low or 3-4 hours on high, while chicken breasts cook in 4-5 hours on low or 2-3 hours on high. Always check that meat reaches safe internal temperatures: 165°F for poultry and 190-203°F for beef stews.

Is Instant Pot slow cooker setting cheaper to run than a traditional slow cooker?

Yes, the Instant Pot slow cooker setting uses slightly less energy than traditional slow cookers because it heats more efficiently, though the savings are minimal over time. However, the real benefit is having one versatile appliance that does both slow cooking and pressure cooking instead of buying two separate devices.

What's the difference between Instant Pot slow cooker low and high settings?

The low setting maintains a temperature of around 190°F and takes about twice as long as high, while high runs at approximately 280°F and cooks food faster. Low is ideal for tougher cuts of meat that benefit from longer cooking, while high works well for faster meals or if you're short on time.
