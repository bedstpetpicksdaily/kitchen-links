What to Look For

  • Capacity of 6-8 quarts: Meal prepping demands volume. A standard 6-quart Instant Pot lets you cook enough chicken, rice, or chili for 4-6 servings in one batch, while an 8-quart model is ideal if you're feeding a family or prepping for multiple people. Anything smaller than 6 quarts means running multiple cycles, which defeats the purpose of efficient meal prep.
  • Stainless steel inner pot with clear measurement markings: Non-stick coating can scratch and wear down after dozens of heavy meal prep sessions. A stainless steel pot withstands years of high-volume cooking and is dishwasher safe, saving you precious cleanup time. Clear measurement markings eliminate guesswork when scaling recipes up for batch cooking.
  • Multiple cooking presets (pressure cook, slow cook, sauté, and sterilize functions): Versatility matters for meal prep variety. You'll want pressure cooking for proteins and grains, slow cook for tougher cuts that benefit from extended cooking, sauté mode for browning meat before pressure cooking, and a sterilize function for safely processing jars of homemade stock or soup.
