What to Look For

  • Cooking Versatility – Consider whether you want a one-trick pony or a multi-function device.   • Time and Learning Curve – Traditional pressure cookers require more hands-on attention and cooking knowledge, especially regarding PSI (pounds per square inch) settings and heat management.   • Counter Space and Storage – Instant Pots are larger appliances that demand permanent or semi-permanent counter real estate due to their multi-function nature. If your kitchen is compact or you prefer minimal clutter, a traditional stovetop pressure cooker takes up minimal storage space and can be tucked away easily after use.
