What to Look For

  • Multiple cooking presets with customization options: For meal prep, you need flexibility to cook everything from dried beans to rice to chicken breasts. Look for models with at least 15+ preset programs and the ability to manually adjust pressure levels and cooking times.   • Stainless steel inner pot with measurement markings: A stainless steel insert (rather than non-stick) is more durable for frequent use and handles acidic ingredients better over time. Clear measurement markings on the inside help you quickly calculate batch sizes without pulling out a separate measuring cup—a small detail that saves serious time during meal prep sessions.
  • Easy-to-read display and one-touch cooking: When you're prepping multiple containers of food, a clear LCD screen and intuitive button layout matter more than you'd think. Models with one-touch programs for popular meal-prep staples like "rice," "poultry," and "bean/chili" reduce decision fatigue and prevent cooking mistakes when you're in a rhythm.

What to Avoid
