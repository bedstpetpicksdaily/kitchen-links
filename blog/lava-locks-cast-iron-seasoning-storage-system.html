Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need to reapply the seasoning with this system?

The wax-based formula lasts longer than oil-based alternatives—typically 2-4 weeks of regular cooking before you'd want to reapply. Heavy cooking (especially acidic foods) reduces this timeline. Most users who bought multiple units report applying it monthly or as-needed when they notice their seasoning looking tired. It's maintenance, not a permanent fix.

Can I use this on newly restored cast iron or only established seasoning?

It works on both, but application differs. On established seasoning, you apply directly. On bare or newly restored iron, you'll want to build a base layer first using your standard seasoning method, then use Lava Locks for maintenance. The wax formula doesn't penetrate bare metal as effectively as a fresh seasoning oil would.

Does this actually prevent rust better than storing cast iron in a cabinet normally?

Yes, measurably so in humid kitchens or coastal areas. The wax barrier is more effective than negligible air exposure at preventing moisture contact. However, if your kitchen stays dry and you're storing cast iron in low-humidity areas, the difference becomes minimal. Test this yourself: if you've never had rust issues with basic storage, you probably don't need this system.
