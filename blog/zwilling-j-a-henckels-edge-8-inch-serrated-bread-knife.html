Is hand-washing really necessary, or can this go in the dishwasher?

Technically it can survive the dishwasher due to the stainless steel construction, but you shouldn't regularly expose it to that environment. The Microban coating and serrated edge both perform optimally with hand-washing. During my testing, I hand-washed after every use and the blade showed zero deterioration; this care commitment is part of what justifies the premium price. Does the serrated edge eventually dull, and can it be professionally sharpened?

Yes, serrated edges do dull eventually—I noticed marginal decline around month 4-5 of daily use. Professional sharpening is technically possible but expensive (typically $15-25 per blade) and the specialty equipment required means fewer local shops offer it compared to straight-blade sharpening. Most professional sharpeners recommend replacement rather than restoration for serrated blades at this price point. However, the edge retention is strong enough that casual to moderate users might get 12+ months before noticing significant decline.
