Frequently Asked Questions

How does the KBG 741 compare to the SCAA-certified Technivorm Moccamaster Classic?

The Classic and KBG 741 are essentially the same machine with minor cosmetic differences. The Classic has a carafe with handle only (no thermal option), while the KBG 741 pairs with the thermal carafe. If you're keeping coffee hot for 30+ minutes, the KBG 741's thermal option justifies the extra $20-30. For 1-2 cup brewing within 10 minutes, the Classic is perfectly adequate.

Is the Moccamaster worth it compared to a $90 OXO Brew or a $60 Black+Decker?

Both OXO and Black+Decker are honest machines that brew at reasonable temperatures and produce drinkable coffee. You'll taste a difference: the Moccamaster produces cleaner, more balanced cup with better clarity, especially with single-origin beans worth $12+ per pound. If you're buying cheap beans, the machine upgrade matters less. If you're spending $80-120 monthly on quality coffee, the Moccamaster is an obvious ROI. If you're buying bulk grocery-store coffee, save the money.

Does the thermal carafe really stay hot enough, or should I use the glass carafe?

The thermal carafe maintains 160-165°F for 4 hours and 150°F+ for 6 hours—genuinely drinkable temperatures without that stale taste from electric warming plates. The glass carafe option exists if you want to see the coffee level, but performance-wise, the thermal carafe is superior. July humidity can affect perception (hot coffee feels less refreshing), but objectively the thermal retention is excellent.

What's the actual brew time, and does it matter?

Expect 4-5 minutes for 10 cups. The Moccamaster deliberately brews slowly to allow complete saturation and extraction. Budget machines rush to 2-3 minutes, which means under-extraction and flat flavor. Yes, it's slower, but the 3-4 minute difference produces measurably better coffee chemistry. Only skip this machine if morning speed is your priority—then honestly, a programmable brewer is more practical.
