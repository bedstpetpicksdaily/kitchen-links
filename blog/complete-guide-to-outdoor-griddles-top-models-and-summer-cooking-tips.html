What Makes Outdoor Griddles Essential for Summer Cooking

Unlike traditional grills with grates, outdoor griddles feature a flat, continuous cooking surface that provides unparalleled versatility. This design allows heat to distribute evenly across the entire surface, making it ideal for cooking multiple items simultaneously at different temperatures. From delicate fish fillets to breakfast foods, griddles handle tasks that would be challenging on a standard grill. The flat surface also minimizes food falling through grates and makes cleanup significantly easier—a major advantage when entertaining large groups throughout the summer season.

Top Summer Cooking Tips for Outdoor Griddles

Maximize your griddle's potential with these expert techniques: preheat thoroughly for 10-15 minutes to ensure even cooking temperatures, season your griddle regularly to build a natural non-stick patina, and invest in quality griddle tools like scrapers and spatulas with longer handles. Master the art of creating temperature zones by utilizing different areas of the cooking surface—keep one side hotter for searing and reserve cooler zones for gentle cooking. For Big Horn Outdoors griddle owners specifically, take advantage of the precise temperature controls to nail perfect results every time. Don't shy away from unconventional foods; griddles excel at cooking items beyond traditional grilling fare, from halved avocados to pound cake slices.

Essential Maintenance and Care

Proper maintenance ensures your outdoor griddle remains a reliable cooking partner for years to come. After each use, scrape the hot surface while it's still warm to remove food debris and buildup. Occasionally deep-clean with a griddle cleaner and steel wool, then dry thoroughly and apply a light coat of oil to prevent rust. Big Horn Outdoors griddles are engineered for durability, but consistent care maximizes their lifespan and performance. Store your griddle with a protective cover during off-season months, and inspect gas lines and connections regularly if you own a gas-powered model.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a griddle and a traditional grill?

Griddles feature a continuous flat cooking surface, while grills have grates. This makes griddles ideal for cooking smaller items, delicate foods, and breakfast items that would fall through grill grates. Griddles also allow for more precise temperature control across different cooking zones.

Are Big Horn Outdoors griddles suitable for gas or charcoal heating?

Big Horn Outdoors manufactures both gas and charcoal-compatible griddles. Gas models offer precise temperature control and quick heating, while charcoal options provide traditional flavor. Choose based on your preferred cooking style and existing outdoor setup.

How often should I season my outdoor griddle?

Season your griddle after deep cleaning, typically 2-3 times per year, or whenever you notice the surface losing its non-stick properties. Regular light oiling after each use also helps maintain the seasoned surface and prevent rust.

What size griddle is best for a family of four?

A 24-30 inch griddle surface provides ample cooking space for a family of four with room for entertaining. Big Horn Outdoors offers multiple size options, so consider your available space and typical entertaining needs when selecting a model.

Can I use my griddle year-round?

Yes, with proper maintenance. Use a protective cover during harsh weather and off-season months. Big Horn Outdoors griddles are built with durable materials designed to withstand various climates, but protecting them extends their lifespan significantly.
