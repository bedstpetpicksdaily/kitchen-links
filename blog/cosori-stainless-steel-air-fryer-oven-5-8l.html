Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 5.8L capacity compare to standard air fryers?

Most compact air fryers max out around 3-4L. The Cosori's 5.8L gives you roughly 40-50% more cooking space, which means you can genuinely cook for four people without rotating batches. I tested this by making chicken thighs for a family of four—fit everything in one go. Standard models would require two separate cooking cycles, negating the speed advantage that makes air fryers appealing.

Is the smart app actually useful, or is it just marketing?

The app exists and technically works, but it's genuinely underwhelming. Remote start sounds convenient until you realize you still need to load the food before you leave, defeating the purpose. Notifications are delayed by 30-60 seconds, which is useless for timing-sensitive cooking. I tested it extensively and reverted to using the physical controls within two weeks. Save yourself the frustration and judge this appliance on its cooking performance alone.

What's the actual cooking quality compared to my regular oven?

For frozen foods and items that benefit from intense circulating heat—fries, wings, frozen vegetables—the air fryer wins decisively. You get crispness without excessive oil and no soggy bottoms. For baking, roasting fresh vegetables, or anything requiring gentle, even heat distribution, the conventional oven still performs better. I tested both for frozen chicken nuggets (air fryer won by a landslide) and fresh herb-roasted potatoes (oven was superior). Use this for what it's designed for, not as a complete oven replacement.
