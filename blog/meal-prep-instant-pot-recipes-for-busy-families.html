What to Look For

  • Larger capacity (8-quart models): Families need volume to meal prep efficiently. An 8-quart Instant Pot lets you cook double or triple portions in a single batch, whereas a 6-quart requires multiple rounds. This matters when you're prepping for 4+ people for an entire week.
  • Multiple cooking functions beyond pressure cooking: Look for models that include slow cook, sauté, and yogurt settings. The sauté function alone saves time by letting you brown meat and vegetables before pressure cooking, building flavor without dirtying extra pans.
  • A reliable, replaceable sealing ring: Meal prep means heavy use. You want a model where the silicone ring is easily replaceable and where you can keep a backup on hand. Food odors linger in these rings, so having extras prevents your chicken dinner from tasting like last week's fish.
